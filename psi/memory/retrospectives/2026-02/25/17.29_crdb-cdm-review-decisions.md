## AI Diary (first-person)
I felt the work snap into place once the ERD review became explicit and mechanical: list every relationship, capture its semantics, and then mark what requires a human decision. The moment the “lingering decisions” section was inserted, the conversation shifted from me guessing the intended modeling philosophy to you directly asserting it in context, in markdown, right next to the model. That made it much easier to propagate changes confidently into the canonical artifact without smuggling assumptions.

I also noticed how often CDM work fails when we try to solve everything at once (entity definitions, cardinalities, and semantics). Breaking it into: (1) relationship inventory, (2) flagged list, (3) decision capture, (4) apply decisions—kept the session grounded. Momentum-wise, this was done on day 1 of an active streak; the pace felt steady rather than rushed, and it helped that we kept all decisions auditable.

## Honest Feedback
1) The original Mermaid ERD mixed naming conventions (`Hazard_Model` vs `HAZARD_MODELS`, `Disaster_Event` vs `HAZARDOUS_EVENT`), which increases the risk of “fixing” the wrong concept. Even small naming inconsistencies amplify confusion in CDM work.

2) Cardinality in Mermaid is easy to misread and easy to encode incorrectly. Without a decision log, it’s tempting to “correct” cardinalities based on best practice rather than actual intended practice.

3) The biggest friction point was attribution semantics: the model can represent attribution as a polymorphic bridge, or as a loss-driven derived record. Both are reasonable, but the downstream consequences are substantial. Capturing the decision explicitly in the artifact was necessary to prevent future reversions.

## Lessons Learned
- A CDM review is more reliable when treated as a *structured decision-capture workflow*, not a freeform edit.
- For multi-method domains (physical vs index-based risk), the model should explicitly support both pathways and encode optionality, instead of forcing one method into the other.
- Attribution is a “claim” that should be anchored to loss evidence; otherwise the CDM encourages speculative causal links.

## Next Steps
- Unify entity naming across drafts and artifact (`DISASTER_EVENT` vs `HAZARDOUS_EVENT`, singular/plural forms).
- If needed, formalize the observed-vs-modeled hazard map distinction as an explicit subtype pattern.
- Extend the same decision-capture approach to remaining domains beyond 1–2 (if the project wants consistent governance of modeling choices).
