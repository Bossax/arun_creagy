# Discussion
- Bank of Thailand (ToB) has engaged with commercial banks to solicit for opinions on the physical risk analysis framework
- The framework for risk assessment and stress testing are common across banks according to BoT, however, the data sources can vary depending on bank's capacity
- Disaster-related data is obtained from DDPM.
- Hydrological hazard and risk data is from Hydro Informatics Insttitute (สสน)
- Bangkok Metropolitan Administration (BMA) has their own data sources and pipelines. They rely on GISTDA spatial data. 
- They prefer to have accessible data repository so that they do not have to go out and acquire data from third party data providers.
- The analysis is to satisfy BoT's requirement and analyze their portfolio exposure. They would love to see if the central data repository can answer to their data granularity requirements , which are usually at asset-level, before taking any decision to procure refined data
- Some flood data requirements they mentioned
	- probability of flooding at a location  
	- multi-modal floods: pluvial, riverine, coastal
	- depth and duration of flooding
- Stress testing requires clearer scenario definitions
- A big gap in risk quantification is damage functions. Now the baks rely on regional study of the damage function, which is too macro and generic. 
- Historical data is also helpful to the banks
- They also prefer probabilistic map over index map
- Provincial level data is not helpful for them 
- Uncertainty handling:
	- Concern about overestimation: when they use, say 100-year flood map of the entire country to assess possible financial impact on the exposure in their portfolio, they have a question whether every region in Thailand will experience 100 year flooding simultaneously in one year. From their perspective, they still treat a probabilistic map as a deterministic map.
	- This implies the need to educate the analysts and standardize uncertainty handling for climate risk assessment 
- **Internal Capital Adequacy Assessment Process (ICAAP)** will include stress testing in their standard
- BoT's scenario still relies on 2011 Thailand's flood event. However, it is just a guideline at this stage.
- The banks are interested in proposing disaster risk reduction measures to their clients. The risk is both direct impact and secondary impact such as supply chain disruption. (They want to know about cascading effects)
- The risk map, if categorized or presented as asset type-aggregate, will be more helpful.
- The mentioned that transition risk is also of great concern but lacks clear guidance at this point
	- they want numeric and clear LT-LEDS pathway of the country that goes down to sub sector to be sufficient for their planning
	- they face issue with industrial categorization: mapping **International Standard Industrial Classification of All Economic Activities** to actual country industrial operations
- KBank uses HII's repeated flooded zone map. they access this data because they registered for data provider with  HII
	- They have: current and near-future flood forecast (based on runoffs map) and future flood based scenarios (use CMP5)
- A person gives opinions that foundational datasets are severely needed and must be provided by DCCE
