## 1) Chapter purpose (1 paragraph)
Chapter 1 must orient a zero-knowledge reader to why a 2590 horizon matters for children and youth in 3 จชต., and establish the report’s scope, definitions, and foresight framing so Chapters 2–4 can operate without re-explaining core terms.

## 2) Reader assumptions and what must be explained from zero
- Assume readers may know the region only through a conflict lens.
- Explain early that the report is child-centred and future-oriented, and that foresight is used for preparedness rather than prediction.
- Explain how to read the report’s logic: signals → drivers → uncertainties → scenarios → strategy and pathways.

## 3) Key messages (3–7)
1) A 2590 horizon is a planning tool to surface long-term constraints and windows of opportunity for child development, not a forecast.
2) In 3 จชต., children and youth pathways are shaped by interacting domains: learning systems, household economy, welfare access, trust, safety, and place-based constraints.
3) Violence/security is treated as context that shapes feasibility and trust; the protagonist is children and youth lived reality.
4) The report uses foresight vocabulary consistently; readers will see a clear chain from evidence to strategy.
5) Evidence hygiene is explicit: unsourced specifics are tracked as HOLD/GAP.

## 4) Required definitions (terms to introduce + preferred Thai wording)
- การกวาดสัญญาณ, สัญญาณ, ปัจจัยขับเคลื่อน, ความไม่แน่นอนสำคัญ, ฉากทัศน์, ภาพอนาคตฐาน, ภาพอนาคตที่พึงประสงค์.
- 3 จังหวัดชายแดนภาคใต้ (3 จชต.).

## 5) Evidence requirements

Claims that must be sourced
- Any baseline numbers about population, education access, welfare coverage, economic indicators.
- Any legally specific description of governance or education arrangements.

Permissible qualitative synthesis
- High-level patterns about compounded constraints and the need for integrated strategy, as long as phrased as synthesis and not presented as numeric facts.
