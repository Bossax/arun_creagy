## 1) Chapter purpose (1 paragraph)
Chapter 5 must synthesize Chapters 1–4 into a policy-relevant close: what the foresight reveals, why it matters for decisions now, what strategic directions follow, what cross-cutting insights recur, and what evidence gaps and next steps remain.

## 2) Reader assumptions and what must be explained from zero
- Assume readers will skim.
- Provide synthesis, not re-teaching foresight tools.
- Ensure the chain from signals → scenarios → strategy is easy to restate.

## 3) Key messages (3–7)
1) The report’s central finding is about compounded constraints and leverage points shaping youth futures in 3 จชต. toward 2590.
2) Scenario thinking clarifies that inaction is a risk trajectory and that robustness matters.
3) Policy implications are about sequencing, feasibility, and trust, not only programme lists.
4) Strategic proposals are organized so decision-makers can act at different levels.
5) Cross-cutting insights highlight conditions that appear across systems and scenarios.
6) The report is transparent about what is known vs what must be strengthened in evidence.

## 4) Required definitions (terms to introduce + preferred Thai wording)
- Keep definitions minimal; reference earlier chapters.
- Use: ความทนทานของยุทธศาสตร์, ประเด็นข้ามมิติ, ข้อเสนอเพื่อการต่อยอด.

## 5) Evidence requirements

Claims that must be sourced
- Any high-stakes claims about effectiveness, feasibility, or quantified impact.

Permissible qualitative synthesis
- Cross-cutting insights derived from scenario patterns and strategy robustness.
