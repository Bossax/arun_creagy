## 1) Chapter purpose (1 paragraph)
Chapter 4 must turn the desirable future into an implementable, scenario-robust strategy: pathways over time, key leverage points, a portfolio of actions, and actor roles, while being explicit about evidence limits and feasibility constraints.

## 2) Reader assumptions and what must be explained from zero
- Readers may expect a list of projects.
- Explain that strategy is a pathway under uncertainty: robust actions, sequencing, and stress-testing against scenarios.
- Explain H1–H2–H3 briefly and use it consistently.

## 3) Key messages (3–7)
1) Strategy is designed to move from baseline constraints toward the desirable future, not to optimize one scenario.
2) Pathways must identify turning points and conditions, not only activities.
3) Leverage points are justified from the system map and Chapter 2 bottlenecks.
4) Robustness is explicit: what works across scenarios vs what is scenario-specific.
5) Portfolio logic mixes quick wins, transitional actions, transformational actions, and protective actions.
6) Actor mapping makes responsibility legible: owner, broker, enabler.

## 4) Required definitions (terms to introduce + preferred Thai wording)
- พยากรณ์ย้อนกลับ, หมุดหมาย, จุดตัดสินใจ.
- กรอบสามขอบฟ้า (H1/H2/H3).
- จุดคานงัด.
- ความทนทานของยุทธศาสตร์.
- พอร์ตโฟลิโอของการดำเนินงาน.
- ผังผู้มีส่วนเกี่ยวข้องและบทบาท.

## 5) Evidence requirements

Claims that must be sourced
- Legal feasibility claims.
- Claims about budget, institutional mandates, or proven effectiveness.

Permissible qualitative synthesis
- Pathway narratives grounded in scenario stress-testing and system bottlenecks.
- Actor roles derived from workshop outputs, phrased cautiously.

## 6) Tables/figures required (and where they come from)
- The canonical strategy table (as in base report) must exist and be internally consistent.
- A short robustness mapping: strategy elements × scenarios.

## 7) Cross-chapter dependencies

Inputs needed from other chapters
- Chapter 3: desirable future and scenario set.
- Chapter 2: leverage points and bottlenecks.

Outputs this chapter promises to later chapters
- Clear portfolio and actor mapping that Chapter 5 can summarize into policy implications.
- Explicit list of remaining evidence gaps that constrain strategy confidence.
