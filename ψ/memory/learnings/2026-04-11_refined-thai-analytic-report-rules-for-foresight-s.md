# Refined Thai analytic-report rules for foresight-style reports: 1) Always define

Refined Thai analytic-report rules for foresight-style reports: 1) Always define a clear central outcome (e.g. quality of life, distribution of opportunity) and write paragraph-level conclusions that point back to that outcome, not only to institutions or systems. 2) Structure the report spine as evidence & signals → system map → key uncertainties → scenarios → strategies → policy implications, and use explicit bridge paragraphs when moving from one layer to the next. 3) Within paragraphs, keep a consistent context → mechanism → consequence pattern, ending with the consequence for people or for the central outcome. 4) Use a disciplined modality ladder: use ‘อาจ’, ‘มีแนวโน้มว่า’ in evidence sections; ‘ชี้ให้เห็นว่า’, ‘สะท้อนว่า’ in synthesis sections; reserve ‘ควร’, ‘จำเป็นต้อง’ for strategy/policy sections with clear justification. 5) Prefer neutral, system-level Thai nouns (e.g. ระบบการเรียนรู้, โครงสร้างเศรษฐกิจครัวเรือน, ความเปราะบางเชิงโครงสร้าง, ความไว้วางใจต่อสถาบันสาธารณะ) and reuse the same term throughout, instead of cycling synonyms for variety. 6) Integrate English technical terms into Thai sentences only on first mention as คำไทยเต็มความหมาย (English term), then use the Thai term as the default. 7) Give each paragraph at most one or two functions (describing evidence, explaining a mechanism, or drawing a systemic implication); if it starts doing more, split it. 8) Pair longer analytic sentences with short summary sentences, to prevent walls of text while keeping analytic density high. 9) Write scenario and future sections in neutral descriptive language, showing why an outcome would be unacceptable through its effects on life chances and justice, rather than by moral labels alone. 10) Treat bullets and numbered lists as carriers of synthesized, actionable propositions (drivers, uncertainties, levers, indicators), not as raw workshop notes.
