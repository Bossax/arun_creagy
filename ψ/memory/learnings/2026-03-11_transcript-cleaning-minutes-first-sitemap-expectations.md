# Learning — Minutes-first synthesis + sitemap-first expectation management

## Pattern

When meeting audio transcription is imperfect, treat **in-room minutes** as the primary source of truth for commitments and intent, and use the transcript as supporting evidence.

For stakeholder alignment, the **sitemap is the control surface** for decision-makers. If leadership equates “website” with “platform,” backend artifacts (CDM, governance gates, product logic) must be narrated as **the reason the sitemap works** (trust, coherence, maintainability), not as separate “more important” deliverables.

If participants ask whether sitemap topics can change later, capture a **sitemap change process** as a first-class deliverable (stable vs flexible, proposal and review/approval flow).

## Why it matters

- Prevents “argument with the transcript” and keeps synthesis aligned with what actually happened in the room.
- Avoids misalignment with leadership expectations by packaging technical work behind the sitemap.
- Turns “can we change it later?” into a governance deliverable rather than an unresolved ambiguity.
