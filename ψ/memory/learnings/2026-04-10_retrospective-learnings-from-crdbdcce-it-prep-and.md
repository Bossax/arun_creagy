# Retrospective learnings from CRDB–DCCE IT prep and Thai writing style work:

Retrospective learnings from CRDB–DCCE IT prep and Thai writing style work:

- Thai internal pre-read notes for inter-department meetings should be structured as: one short objective paragraph + ≤5 main headings + 1–3 short bullets per heading framed as topics/questions, not full analytical prose.
- Use real institutional names and Thai phrasing the team actually uses (e.g. โครงการพัฒนาโครงสร้างข้อมูลด้านการปรับตัว, กวส.), keeping English only for unavoidable technical terms such as data catalog.
- Shift from a “requirements / non‑negotiables” tone into a “joint questions and decision points” tone inside the pre-read; keep hard architectural conditions in separate background memos.
- Separate surfaces: pre-read for what to talk about and what answers are needed; deeper architecture/governance logic lives in dedicated notes (integration notes, implementation plans, decision logs).
- /writing-th + /writing-th-learn pairing works best when file naming/mapping (draft vs edited) is explicit; otherwise, learning extraction becomes manual and brittle.

Implication: for CRDB/CRI work, when preparing Thai pre-read notes for DCCE IT or similar counterparts, draft with this structure and tone from the start, and then run a quick learn-back after human edits so future drafts align more closely with internal style without over-specifying architecture in the pre-read itself.
