# When analyzing climate-service use cases, avoid treating “typology” as a single 

When analyzing climate-service use cases, avoid treating “typology” as a single labeling exercise.

Different typology lenses answer different design questions, so mixing them without stating the intent creates drift:

- Service-format typology (Maps & Apps / Expert Analysis / Climate-inclusive Consulting / Sharing Practices) answers: How is this delivered as a service (operating model, staffing, governance, automation potential)?
- Decision-process depth answers: What maturity of information bundle is required (screening vs analysis vs options), and what enablers are prerequisite across all stages?
- Information-content typology (Jagannathan detailed vs trends vs guidance, incl. sub-types) answers: What kinds of climate information content are demanded (metrics/events/drivers vs trends vs improvement/guidance)?

Rule of thumb: every typology mapping must state (1) the lens, (2) the decision it supports, and (3) whether assignments are explicitly tagged vs inferred from prose.
