# Lesson Learned — CRDB interim report needs progress-first Section 1 logic

When drafting an interim report for NCAIF/CRDB, do not lead with a static list of artifacts. Instead, structure the chapter as a progress-first explanation of how the team arrived at the current architecture: stakeholder interviews establish user demand, conceptual data modeling explains the need for a management structure, standards and literature alignment prevent semantic drift, T-PLAT provides a national precedent and caution, and current-state evidence (website gap, risk-map constraints, UX/UI principles) justifies the Phase 1 sitemap and MVP boundaries. This makes CDM the hidden organizing logic and MVPs the visible, bounded service expressions, which is far easier for sponsors to understand and defend.
