# Foresight rewrite must separate gap analysis, synthesis spine, rewrite, and grounding

## Pattern
When revising a large Thai strategic report with mixed team input, draft fragments, and evolving citation quality, a single-pass rewrite causes confusion. The stable pattern is:

1. Diagnose the section’s function and gaps.
2. Create a short synthesis spine that locks logic, paragraph order, and emphasis.
3. Rewrite the prose from that spine.
4. Ground and cite only after the prose stabilizes.

## Why it matters
- It prevents sections from turning into rehearsals of previous chapters.
- It keeps section objectives distinct.
- It reduces churn when users refine structure, headings, or descriptor logic mid-process.
- It keeps citation work from corrupting drafting flow.

## Specific takeaways from this session
- System-map sections must foreground nodes, variable directions, and loops, not just narrative restatement.
- Baseline, persona, scenarios, desirable future, and strategy blocks each need different rhetorical jobs.
- Descriptor naming must be concrete and one-function-per-descriptor.
- Temporary numeric citations are a practical bridge when bibliography metadata is incomplete.

## Reuse conditions
Use this pattern whenever a report:
- has strong structural dependencies between chapters,
- mixes workshop logic with evidence-backed material,
- or is being revised interactively with human feedback on section function.
