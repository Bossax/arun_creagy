# Learning — Product-Led Co-creation and Knowledge Equalization

The primary value of a multi-stakeholder climate coordination workshop is the removal of **Information Asymmetry**. By creating a **"Common Operating Picture"** of the national data landscape, we enable coordination that is impossible in a siloed environment.

### Key Patterns:
- **The "Mirror" Effect**: High-efficiency engagement is achieved by presenting stakeholders with a map of what is already known (The Mirror) and asking for corrections/gaps, rather than asking them to list data from zero.
- **Product-Led Grouping**: Organizing participants around an **Ideal Product Concept** (e.g., a Strategic Briefing Suite) ensures they are personally invested and provides a concrete anchor for technical workflow testing.
- **The Skeleton vs. Co-creation Balance**: Provide a functional "skeleton" of a workflow (based on internal MVPs) to maintain technical precision, but leave enough "white space" for stakeholders to provide the "flesh"—the real-world constraints, policies, and specific datasets.
- **WMO NFCS Legitimacy**: Using international standard roles (Data Providers, Co-producers, Intermediaries, End-Users) provides a robust framework for table grouping and ensures all parts of the climate service value chain are represented.
- **Plain Language Mandate**: Success in co-production depends on bridging the gap between technical "Makers" and policy "Users" by replacing project jargon with action-oriented, everyday language.
