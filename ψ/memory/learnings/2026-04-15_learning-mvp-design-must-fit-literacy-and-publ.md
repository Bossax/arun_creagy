# Learning — MVP design must fit literacy and publishing risk

**Date**: 2026-03-06
**Context**: CRDB/NCAIF Phase 1 MVP refinement for FGD2.

## Pattern

When the user base is mixed/low literacy and published outputs carry reputational/legal risk (especially forecasts and probabilistic layers), “MVP” cannot mean self-service analysis tools.

Instead, Phase 1 MVPs should be designed as:

1) **Tiered dissemination**
   - **Tier 1**: prescriptive, export-first “briefing packs” operated/curated by a central team.
   - **Tier 2**: advanced layers/registries for trained analysts with explicit semantics.

2) **Publishing safety as a product requirement**
   - limitations + uncertainty statement required
   - draft → review → publish workflow (or an explicit SOP)
   - named owners (domain + comms/PR + governance)

3) **Catalog-first/link-first architecture**
   - avoid duplicating national data infrastructure; add value through endorsement, metadata, and guidance.

4) **Post-event data as quarantine + revision**
   - model lag/noise, validation flags, and revision history; separate “relief payouts” from “estimated true loss.”

## Why it matters

- Prevents “weaponization by misunderstanding” and reduces institutional fear of publishing.
- Preserves usability for local planners while still supporting advanced analyst needs.
- Keeps Phase 1 feasible and defensible in workshops.
