# Thai internal meeting pre-read notes (CRDB → DCCE IT) – editing patterns from dr

Thai internal meeting pre-read notes (CRDB → DCCE IT) – editing patterns from draft→edited:

1) Scope and purpose
- Treat this as a short “โน้ตเตรียมตัว” (pre-read), not a full analytical memo.
- Open with one concise paragraph on meeting objective (what decision/clarity is needed), remove extra meta framing (e.g. session length, execution spine narratives).

2) Level of detail
- Keep the main section headings (context, IT project status, architecture/platform, governance/roles, key questions).
- Inside each section, cut deep architecture logic (Thaiwater slots, rails, governance gates) and save it for separate reference notes.
- For pre-read, a few bullets per section are enough; each bullet should be a prompt for room discussion, not a full explanation.

3) Tone and posture
- Shift from “requirements/non‑negotiables” tone to “joint questions” tone.
- Replace language that sounds like fixed conditions with questions such as “โครงการพร้อมจะใช้ผลผลิตในประเด็นใดบ้าง” and “มีข้อจำกัดด้านเทคนิคหรือกรอบเวลาอะไรที่ต้องคำนึงถึง”.

4) Wording and structure
- Use Thai institutional names that the team actually uses (เช่น “โครงการพัฒนาโครงสร้างข้อมูลด้านการปรับตัว”, “กวส.”) instead of only CRDB/Phase‑1 labels.
- Prefer short bullets with clear action/subject (“แลกเปลี่ยนเรื่องการจัดทำแบบจำลองข้อมูล”, “คำถามเกี่ยวกับบทบาทหน้าที่”) over long multi-clause sentences.
- Use Thai terms by default (เช่น “รูปแบบการเผยแพร่ข้อมูล”, “มาตรฐานข้อมูลอ้างอิง”) and keep English only for unavoidable technical terms (e.g. data catalog).

5) What gets cut from the draft
- Remove detailed post‑meeting workflow (memo drafting, document packs, follow-up sessions) from the pre-read; keep focus on what will be discussed and what answers are needed in the meeting.

Implication for future drafts: when drafting Thai pre-read notes for internal meetings with DCCE IT or similar counterparts, start with the full analytical framing if needed, then deliberately compress into: (a) one objective paragraph, (b) ≤5 main headings, (c) 1–3 short Thai bullets per heading framed as topics and questions, with heavier architecture/governance logic moved to separate background notes.
