# Governance-led SETS mapping for urban resilience indices

When designing a municipal-level climate resilience index (CRI), focusing on functional governance pillars—the "Institutional Readiness" lens—is more policy-relevant and implementable than broad ecological or social frameworks alone.

## Pattern
1. **SETS as the Umbrella**: Use the Social-Ecological-Technological Systems (SETS) framework to ensure conceptual completeness (Social, Ecological, Technological, Institutional domains).
2. **Governance as the Multiplier**: Frame the measurement around **Functional Pillars** (Planning, Finance, HR, Coordination, etc.) within the Institutional domain. These are the "levers" that decision-makers (Mayors/Governors) actually control to activate assets in other domains.
3. **The "Administrative Anchor"**: To bridge the gap between high-level concepts (e.g., "Risk-informed planning") and actual implementation, anchor each indicator to a specific **Thai Administrative Trace** (e.g., LPA codes, budget reports, or digital platform logs).

## Why it works
- **Actionability**: It identifies gaps in specific departments rather than assigning a generic "vulnerability" score.
- **Feasibility**: Administrative traces (secondary data) are already structured by governance responsibility, making the index scalable across many jurisdictions (e.g., 76 provinces) without primary data collection.
- **Scientifically Validated**: Aligning functional levers with agent capacities (responsiveness, resourcefulness, learning) reflects the modern evolution of resilience theory.
