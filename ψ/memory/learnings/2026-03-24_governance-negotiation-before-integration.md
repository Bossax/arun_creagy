# Governance negotiation before integration

In CRDB planning, knowledge that line agencies possess internal datasets, models, or workflows must not be translated into assumptions of access, transparency, or trust. Integration framing should begin from governance uncertainty: what can be seen, what can be shared, what can be endorsed, and what remains opaque. Workshops are not only validation mechanisms for architecture; they are also arenas for surfacing reluctance, negotiation boundaries, and institutional comfort levels around data, models, and analysis results.
