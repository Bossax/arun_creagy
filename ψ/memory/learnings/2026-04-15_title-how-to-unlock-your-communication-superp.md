## Key takeaways (draft)
- Great conversations are **messy**; the point isn’t “say the right thing” but **create mutual understanding**.
- “Matching” matters: in good conversations, people’s **emotions / energy / participation** begin to synchronize.
- Most conversations include (or shift between) **three layers**:
  - **Practical**: plans, decisions, problem solving
  - **Emotional**: how we feel; validation; safety
  - **Social**: identity/status/belonging; how we relate
- “Super communicators” aren’t born; they **learn** skills—especially **asking more questions**.
- “Deep questions” act like invitations that create psychological safety and openness.
- For high-conflict conversations, a concrete tool is **looping**: ask a deep question, reflect back what you heard, and confirm.
