# Learning: Methodology Operationalization and the "Artifact Rule"

**Date**: 2026-04-27
**Context**: Upgrading CRI Phase 2 methodology from v3/v4 to v5 Worksheet Edition.
**Concept**: The Artifact Rule / Process-Aware Governance / Forensic Evidence Tracing

## The Pattern (The "Ghost in the Machine")
In complex consultancy and research projects, there is a recurring failure pattern where "Hardened Knowledge" generated during experiments or analysis (e.g., side-notes, interpretation logs, discussion memos) fails to reach the **primary operational artifact** (e.g., the dictionary, the scoring rubric, the worksheet). 

This creates a "Ghost in the Machine"—the analyst (AI or human) *thinks* they are using the latest logic, but the tool they are using still carries the old, static definitions.

## Key Insights

### 1. The Artifact Rule
Narrative notes and "side-cars" do not change behavior. Methodology only becomes real when it is "baked into" the canonical work-surface. In this session, the transition from a **Dictionary** (definitions) to a **Worksheet** (operational instructions) was the mechanism that forced the hardening of the definitions.

### 2. The Fail-Safe Process Boundary
A critical methodological realization: You cannot judge "Fail-Safe" capacity by looking at **Asset Stocks** (the pipe, the dam). Safe-to-fail is a **dynamic property of Governance Activation**. Therefore, evidence for robustness must shift from *Inventory* (stock-evidence) to *Discipline* (process-evidence, e.g., maintenance frequency, test logs).

### 3. Forensic Evidence Tracing vs. Category Overload
Internal versioning tags (e.g., "v2 cluster") lead to "Expert Drift" and informational decay. Professional-grade methodology requires **Forensic Traceability** back to primary literature (IPCC, OECD, Brito et al. 2026). This restores the authority of the framework and prevents it from becoming an "internal echo chamber."

### 4. Action-Aware Descriptions
Definitions should be written as **Activation Chains** (`Asset → Governance → Function`). This forces the analyst to identify specific **bottlenecks** (e.g., "the pump exists but the protocol is missing") rather than providing generic "readiness" scores.

## Application in the Fleet
- **Audit Rule**: Always perform a "Side-by-Side Audit" of new versions against old ones to ensure that experimental findings have materially changed the definitions.
- **Structural Rule**: Use multi-column Worksheets to enforce evidence discipline (e.g., mandatory 'evidence' and 'example' columns).
- **persona Rule**: Treat "best guesses" as hallucinations until verified by the traced citation.
