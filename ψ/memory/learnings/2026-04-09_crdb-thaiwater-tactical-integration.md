# Learning: Tactical Integration of Thaiwater-inspired Governance

**Date**: 2026-04-09
**Context**: CRDB Project — Enhancing implementation plans with Thaiwater standards.
**Tags**: #governance #crdb #thaiwater #metadata #qc

## The Pattern
When moving from high-level data governance concepts (like the Thaiwater model) to project-specific execution plans, it is most effective to embed requirements directly into **scheduled stakeholder engagement streams**. Instead of treating "governance" as a separate deliverable, it becomes the **agenda and artifact set** for upcoming meetings.

## Application in CRDB
In this session, we converted the "Thaiwater Enhancement Concept Note" into specific requirements for three engagement streams:
1. **IT Discussion (Architecture):** Translated generic standards into concrete requirement briefs (reference layers, observation envelopes).
2. **Consultation Workshop (Evidence):** Structured inventories to capture specific metadata fields (QC flags, units, agency codes) identified in the concept note.
3. **FGD3 (Commitment):** Framed governance control flows (onboarding, revision, publication) as internal DCCE ownership decisions.

## Why this works
1. **Actionability:** It forces the conceptual model to fit into the practical limits of a meeting or a spreadsheet.
2. **Alignment:** It ensures that stakeholders (IT managers, external agencies, DCCE directors) are confronted with specific technical and governance choices rather than abstract principles.
3. **Traceability:** The "governance evidence" is built into the workshop outputs and meeting minutes, satisfying TOR requirements (5.2.3, 5.3.9) by default.

## Next Step Implementation
The pattern will be validated by the creation of the **"CRDB → IT requirements brief"** and **"Pre-seeded inventory sheets"**, which will serve as the tangible interface between the Thaiwater concepts and the CRDB stakeholders.
