## Learning

When a CRDB project-management artifact becomes hard to use, the fix is usually not “more content” but **better surfacing**:

1) Add a short **Status (as of DATE)** block at the top (3–6 bullets) linking to the *latest anchors* (meeting decisions, submission snapshots, and sponsor watchpoints).
2) Add a **Now / next / later (actionable)** section that translates those anchors into a small checklist, with one link per action.
3) Preserve the rest of the document as the durable reference layer (objectives, decisions, outputs) to avoid rewriting history.

This improves readability and discoverability (one-screen “where we are”), and makes the plan operational (explicit next actions), without destabilizing the underlying structure.

## Why this matters

- PM artifacts are used under time pressure; “find the right anchor fast” is the core function.
- Explicit action lists reduce repeated re-derivation of next steps from scattered notes.
