## The Pattern
The "Expert Drift" in urban resilience frameworks often stems from using academic taxonomies (like SETS: Social-Ecological-Technological Systems) as the primary interface for local stakeholders. While theoretically comprehensive, SETS creates "Operational Invisible Silos" because local governments budget and manage by **Functional Mandates (ภารกิจ)** rather than the "nature" of the asset.

### Key Insights:
1.  **Mandate Mapping**: Indicators must mirror the organizational structure of the user. For a Thai Local Administrative Organization (อปท.), grouping by "Public Works," "Public Health," and "Finance" is more actionable than grouping by "Ecological" vs. "Technological" stocks.
2.  **The Linguistic Bridge**: Technical terms like "Ecological Stock" must be translated into "Operational Thai" (e.g., "Green Areas and Shade") to ensure officials can find their own work in the index.
3.  **The SFW Nexus (Solid Waste-Flood-Wastewater)**: Solid Waste Management is not merely a hygiene service; it is a critical **physical constraint** on flood adaptation. Uncollected waste cclogs drainage infrastructure, neutralizing "gray" investments. A resilient city must treat "Waste & Environmental Hygiene" as a standalone resilience system.
4.  **Nested Taxonomies**: To maintain theoretical rigor without overwhelming stakeholders, use a 2-axis maturity matrix:
    *   **Axis 1 (Functional)**: Service Systems (Buckets) - Answers "Who handles this?".
    *   **Axis 2 (Qualitative)**: Resilience Capacities (Filters) - Answers "How well is it handled?" (Absorptive, Adaptive, Transformative).

## Implementation
When designing local resilience indices:
1.  **Audit the Mandate**: Start with the user's job description and budget lines.
2.  **Group by Service**: Use "Cooling," "Water Defense," and "Utility Continuity" as the primary categories.
3.  **Lens SETS**: Use SETS as an internal "Completeness Check" to ensure each service system integrates social, ecological, and technological dimensions.
4.  **Embed Penalties**: Convert equity "notes" into hard scoring penalties (e.g., "Digital Divide" penalty for smartphone-only warning systems).
