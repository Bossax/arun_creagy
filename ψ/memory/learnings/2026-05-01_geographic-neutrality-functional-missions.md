# Learning: Geographic Neutrality via Functional Missions

## Pattern: The Geographic Adoption Wall
In urban resilience frameworks, categorizing assets by their specific physical form (e.g., "Seawalls" or "Check-dams") creates a "Geographic Adoption Wall" where officials in non-relevant areas (e.g., landlocked or plain cities) ignore the framework.

## Solution: Functional Mission-Based Pillars
Shift the taxonomy from **form-based** to **mission-based**. Group assets by their role in the resilience lifecycle rather than their physical nature.

### The 6 Mission Pillars:
1. **Continuity (P1)**: Metabolism/Stability (Water, Energy, Food, Mobility).
2. **Coping (P2)**: Event Absorption/Protection (Hazard Hardware + Relief).
3. **Transformation (P3)**: Structural Evolution/Safe-to-Fail (Nature-Based Solutions + Zoning).
4. **Intelligence (P4)**: Awareness/Early Warning (Sensors + Decision Platforms).
5. **Readiness (P5)**: Agency/Social Capital (Volunteer Networks + Human Capability).
6. **Buffer (P6)**: Elasticity/Economic Capital (Financial Stocks + Diversity).

## Key Learning
The *role* of an asset is more stable than its *form*. By using "Coping Hardware" as a category, a coastal city can plug in a "Seawall" and a mountain city can plug in a "Debris Barrier" without changing the underlying scoring logic or methodology. This ensures **Geographic Neutrality** while maintaining **Structural Symmetry** with Governance pillars ($M = A \times G$).
