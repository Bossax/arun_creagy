# Thai gov-style invitation drafting — CRDB workshop

Thai gov-style invitation drafting (CRDB workshop):

## Patterns

1) **Reader-first invitations**
- Invitation letters must lead with project background + why it matters + what participants/organizations will get.
- Meeting mechanics and facilitation methods belong in internal facilitation notes, not external-facing attachments.

2) **Use TOR-aligned vocabulary as the safe backbone**
- Prefer consistent TOR terms: **ชุดข้อมูล**, **ผลิตภัณฑ์สารสนเทศ**, **ความต้องการใช้งาน**.
- Avoid invented consultant terms that are not standard in Thai government correspondence.

3) **Avoid robotic emphasis**
- Avoid quote-mark stressing, informal catchphrases, and unnecessary English in formal Thai attachments.
- Treat agenda formatting and idiom as correctness conditions, not decoration.

4) **Ask for non-token participation without saying it**
- Do not mention “tokenism” explicitly.
- Specify representative capabilities instead:
  - understands datasets/products + constraints
  - can reflect real needs
  - can coordinate follow-up

5) **Make prep questions answerable**
- Prefer prompts about current practice, bottlenecks, and 1–2 actionable suggestions.
- Scope dataset list requests so they are not interpreted as exhaustive inventories.
