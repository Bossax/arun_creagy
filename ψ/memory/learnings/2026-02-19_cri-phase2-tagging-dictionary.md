# Lesson Learned — CRI Phase 2 tagging dictionary (2026-02-19)

## Core Lesson

For CRI Phase 2, a defensible capacity‑tagging approach requires treating **tagging as its own design object**, not just a hidden step in the methodology. That means:

- Articulating explicit tagging rules (process vs stock, mandate‑aligned naming, readiness modifiers).
- Building a **nominal indicator long‑list** that is small but clearly tied to concrete administrative events (plan updates, coordination meetings, procurement cycles, service delivery times).
- Linking each indicator to at least one publication, website, or named case study, so that every row in the dictionary has a visible evidence trail.

This shifts the conversation with DCCE from “trust our expert tagging” to “here is the rule‑book and its references,” which is far easier to defend in a political review of the index.

## Implications for CRI

- The tagging dictionary should be treated as a living artifact, updated as new sectoral case studies and Thai‑specific governance examples are added.
- Other CRI documents should reference the dictionary explicitly to avoid drift in how Coping/Adaptive/Transformative capacities are operationalized.
