These clusters connect to the current gaps by showing that CRDB already has substantial non-archived evidence on architecture, portal/product shortcomings, stakeholder demand, and governance questions, but the transition from synthesis to execution is blocked by unresolved ownership, endorsement, and operating-model decisions.

The main missing information still needed is:

- named owner, steward, custodian, reviewer, and approver roles for priority topics
- baseline endorsement and supersession rules by topic
- topic-specific boundaries for hosted, linked, mirrored, interpreted, or internal data
- workshop validation targets for baselines, access rules, granularity needs, and service tiers
- shorter management-ready evidence packs that convert the current synthesis into explicit asks, decisions, and validation prompts
