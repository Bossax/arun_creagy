## Lessons Learned

- When a planning note becomes the de facto execution spine (like the three-stream work-steps plan), it must appear explicitly in the trigger log, change log, deliverable map, and claim register; otherwise the project history under-represents the weight of that decision.
- It is cleaner to treat “IT discussion → workshop → FGD3” as a single staged instrument rather than three separate meetings; this simplifies how future notes explain dependencies, evidence flow, and governance ratification.
- Aligning new plans with the interim report’s Chapter 1 narrative before touching ledgers avoids contradictions later; the history is already written there and should be treated as a constraint, not a suggestion.
