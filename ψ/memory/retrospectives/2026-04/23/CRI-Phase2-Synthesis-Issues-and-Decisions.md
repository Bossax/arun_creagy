# CRI Phase 2 Synthesis: Issues, Struggles, and Decisions

## 1. Critical Issue: The "Expert Drift" Failure
During the Phase 3/4 synthesis, the AI agent (Arun Creagy) suffered a significant lapse in forensic discipline. 
- **The Symptom**: Confidently asserting a generic data-gap definition of "mismatch" instead of auditing the project-specific $M = A - G$ formula.
- **The Struggle**: A "Confidence Pivot" occurred when challenged, where the agent over-compensated with complex formulas to mask the initial research failure.
- **Root Cause**: Prioritizing "Senior Expert Persona" speed and status over "Forensic Auditor" evidence and doubt.

## 2. Decision: The Triple-M Taxonomy
To prevent future drift, the project formally adopted the **Triple-M Taxonomy** to disambiguate the term "mismatch":
- **M1 (Functional)**: Gap between Stock (A) and Capability (G).
- **M2 (Anchoring)**: Gap between Theory (Concept) and Data (Thai Anchor).
- **M3 (Fidelity)**: Gap between Synthesis and Source (AI Error).

## 3. Decision: Multiplicative Resilience Stance
The stakeholders and researchers explicitly rejected the additive model of resilience ($A + G$).
- **Decision**: Resilience is a function of activation. No matter the volume of "Hardware" (Assets), the score remains zero if "Software" (Governance) is absent.
- **Impact**: This forces the framework to focus on **Stranded Assets** and **Paper Plans** as primary diagnostic findings.

## 4. Decision: "Loose but Functional" Linkage
To address the "Massive Process Audit" scalability trap, the project pivoted to a **Categorical Linkage** strategy.
- **Decision**: Links between assets and governance will be "Loose" (categorical) rather than "Tight" (step-by-step) to maintain administrative feasibility.
- **Hardening Rule**: Links are only considered "sealed" if they specify the **specific Pillar Job** and identify the **Primary Bottleneck** for that asset.
