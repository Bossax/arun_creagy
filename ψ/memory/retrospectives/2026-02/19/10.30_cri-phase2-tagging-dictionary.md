## Honest Feedback

1. The indicator long‑list is still relatively short and skewed toward governance and financial processes. It does not yet fully reflect sectoral nuances (e.g., water, health, housing) or Thailand‑specific administrative realities. That will need another research pass.
2. Evidence anchors are currently at publication or website level, not precise page/figure references. For political defense, you may eventually want more granular citations or short quote snippets to show that each indicator is clearly grounded in prior work.
3. The integration back into the CRI ecosystem is partial: the methodology now references the tagging dictionary, but other artifacts (e.g., implementation plan, working status brief, data model docs) still speak about tagging in more generic terms. There is a residual risk of divergence if those files are not updated in a later pass.

## Lessons Learned

- A small, well‑defined tagging dictionary with explicit rules and citations is more defensible than a long, vaguely justified indicator list.
- Splitting research prompts by tool (general web vs. research search) helps keep questions sharp and prevents duplicated effort.
- Treating governance‑readiness, coordination, and data quality as first‑class indicators is both feasible with administrative data and consistent with current urban resilience literature.
