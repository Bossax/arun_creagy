## Honest Feedback (100+ words, 3 friction points)
1. The friction around command execution (WSL vs Git Bash vs cmd) still breaks flow. Even though tools like `tools/gitbash.cmd` exist, the quoting is finicky enough that it can derail a reflection workflow. A more robust, pre-tested wrapper for /rrr and /forward would pay dividends.
2. The split between plan files, execution checklists, handoff notes, and retros is powerful but cognitively heavy. It’s easy to lose track of which file is the “current truth” for a given thread of work. Some lightweight index or “current focus” pointer could reduce that friction.
3. On the CRI side, there’s still a conceptual gap between what the literature says we should measure (process, friction, learning) and what the data actually exposes. That misalignment keeps surfacing as doubt when proposing metrics. Building a dedicated “data reality check” step earlier in the workflow might help.

## Lessons Learned
- A well-designed indicator mapping table is a powerful lens: it reveals not just what you are measuring, but what you are *not* measuring, and forces an explicit conversation about process vs stock.
- Handoffs work best when they embed your doubts and constraints (like data unavailability), not just your accomplishments; otherwise the next session has to rediscover the same limitations.
- Workflow tools such as /forward and /rrr become much more valuable when they are tightly integrated with project-specific artifacts (plans, checklists, critiques) rather than used as generic summaries.
