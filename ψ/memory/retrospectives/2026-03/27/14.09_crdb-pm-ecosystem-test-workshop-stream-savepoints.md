When the idea note became Evidence → Trigger → Claim → Deliverable, it stopped being a loose thought and became something we can execute and trace. Even with that, I still felt the mild discomfort that “architecture work” can become a procrastination shelter—so I tried to end with save-points that create forward motion rather than just definitions.

## Honest Feedback (3 friction points)
1) **Artifact overload and user trust**: creating new orientation documents can feel like busywork when there are already Hub/plan/evidence systems. The only safe version is one that is clearly derivative, minimal, and linked from canonical navigation; otherwise it increases confusion.
2) **Tooling mismatch (dig/pulse)**: `/dig` could not reconstruct session timelines because `/c/Users/sitth/.claude/projects` was not present, and pulse files were stale. That forces reliance on git + retrospectives and increases the chance of missing non-committed conversational context.
3) **Semantic ambiguity in filenames**: the phrase “progress meeting decisions” can mean (a) what was decided in the meeting, or (b) what to talk about in the meeting. That ambiguity matters because the PM ledgers depend on clean “planned vs outcome” distinctions.

## Lessons Learned
- New “orientation” artifacts should only be created if they are (1) explicitly derivative, and (2) immediately wired into canonical navigation surfaces.
- The PM ecosystem works best when it enforces semantic separation between: pre-meeting positioning, meeting outcomes, and downstream execution deliverables.
- For the workshop stream, the fastest path to execution is to materialize **D-006** as a real output artifact (invite inputs + agenda + breakout prompts), rather than letting workshop planning stay as scattered notes.
