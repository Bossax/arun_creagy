## AI Diary (150+ words)
I felt the session stretch into a true debugging workout. I started by chasing the obvious surface symptoms—mode not selecting, no responses coming back—but I had to slow down and work methodically, validating each step of the automation pipeline. Once the safer mode-selection logic worked, it was clear the real friction lived elsewhere: the output itself. Discovering that Deep Research writes into a canvas panel with a Share & Export workflow forced me to reframe how “response” should be captured. I felt a mix of frustration and clarity as I realized the existing selectors were doomed to fail, and that the right approach had to be a UI-action pathway plus clipboard fallback. I also noticed my own tendency to over-trust short-lived subscriptions; adding persistent response handling made the system more resilient. There was a sense of relief when the output was finally captured and saved into the inbox, because it validated the hypothesis and turned a brittle workflow into a repeatable one. On day one of this streak, I felt the quiet confidence of building a more reliable bridge between the automation and the human-readable artifact.

## Honest Feedback (100+ words, 3 friction points)
Friction point 1: The system’s response retrieval was overly optimistic and relied on transient subscriptions, which created a false impression of missing data and wasted cycles. I need to default to persistent listeners or retained topics when working with long-running automations. Friction point 2: The UI assumptions in the extension were too strict, and the initial selector strategy failed silently. A more defensive, observable selector strategy should have been the baseline from the start. Friction point 3: The workflow had too many overlapping terminals and processes, which made it hard to tell which results were authoritative; I should consolidate or explicitly terminate long-running terminals after use. Despite these frictions, the session closed with a working canvas extraction pathway and a saved report, but the experience highlighted how quickly UI automation can drift without robust logging and fallbacks.

## Lessons Learned
- Deep Research output is delivered via a canvas panel; response capture must use the Share & Export copy flow or a canvas-aware DOM extraction.
- Robust UI automation needs resilient selectors, retries, and visible debug logging.

## Next Steps
- Consider adding a dedicated `get_canvas` shortcut in the client workflow docs.
- Reduce terminal clutter by standardizing start/stop commands for long-running scripts.
- Validate the saved report for completeness and run a second extraction if needed.
