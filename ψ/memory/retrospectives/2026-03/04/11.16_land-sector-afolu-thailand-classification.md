## AI Diary (first-person)

I focused on turning a set of partially overlapping definitions into something operational. The biggest risk in this topic is category drift: “forest” means one thing in IPCC AFOLU (a land-use category linked to thresholds and land-use intent), another thing in FAO reporting, and yet another thing in Thai law where the definition can be tenure-based. If I don’t make that mismatch explicit, the analysis becomes misleading even when each sentence is locally true.

I tried to keep the note honest by separating what we *know* from what we’re *guessing*. That’s why I added a final verdict section with defaults (rubber → Cropland; shaded coffee → Cropland agroforestry) while still flagging the missing piece: Thailand’s actual GHG inventory mapping rules. I also treated Thailand’s watershed class / permanent forest criteria as an important constraint: it doesn’t automatically decide IPCC category, but it strongly affects how conversion should be represented and why some “agriculture under trees” cases should be surfaced as conversion rather than quietly re-labelled.

3) “Final verdict” is always a trade: it unblocks decisions but can be over-trusted. The note needed strong scope language and a clear superseding condition (find Thailand inventory definition), otherwise it risks becoming sticky lore.

## Lessons Learned

- When local law defines “forest” by **tenure**, treat it as a separate axis from IPCC AFOLU’s **inventory land-use classification**, and add an explicit mapping section rather than blending terms.
- For perennial tree crops + agroforestry, a pragmatic tie-breaker is: default to **Cropland** unless the inventory explicitly classifies the system as Forest Land, and treat conservation-zoned agricultural use as **conversion** if it occurs.
