Emotionally, this felt like librarian work: less creative, more precise. I like that. The concrete outcome is a better map of dependencies and failure modes, and that reduces future uncertainty.

## Honest Feedback
Three friction points stood out:

1) The `/watch` failure surface is too wide for a single “it failed” message. In the log, multiple warnings/errors appear (missing `mosquitto_*`, missing JS runtime, captions ambiguity). Without a structured “primary cause vs secondary warnings” section, it’s easy to fix the wrong thing first.

2) Captions are a UX trap. The diagnostic reports “Available automatic captions” and then concludes “has no subtitles.” That is technically consistent (manual subtitles absent) but practically confusing. The workflow should explicitly decide whether it wants manual subtitles, auto-captions, or either.

3) The environment is drifting. `psi/data/pulse/*.json` exists and updates, but key metrics are currently zeros. That means the signal we expect to read during `/rrr` is not yet trustworthy, and retrospectives can’t lean on it.

## Lessons Learned
- `/watch` has three hard dependencies that should be checked in-order: MQTT client tools (mosquitto), yt-dlp extraction ability (JS runtime), and caption availability/mode (subs vs auto-subs).
- A diagnostic log path is a first-class artifact. Keeping it stable at [`output/watch/_diag.log`](output/watch/_diag.log:1) makes it easy to reference in retrospectives and future debugging.
- Pulse metrics are only useful if they are populated consistently; otherwise retrospectives should treat them as “presence only,” not truth.
