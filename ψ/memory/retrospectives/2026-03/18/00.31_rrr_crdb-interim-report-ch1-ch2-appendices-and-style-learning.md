## Honest Feedback
This was a productive session, but it also exposed three friction points clearly. First, I still have a tendency to externalize structure into extra documents too quickly when a user may prefer more logic to stay embedded inside an anchor file. That is not catastrophic, but it creates avoidable cleanup and makes the workflow feel heavier than it needs to. Second, I drafted some Thai report prose in a way that was directionally correct but not yet grounded enough in the user’s preferred tone. The user comments helped, but ideally I should have anticipated the need for more natural Thai administrative phrasing and more operational examples earlier. Third, there was session-discipline drag caused by repeated mode shifts, approvals, and skill workflows. The structure protected quality, but it also stretched the work over many turns. I can feel that tension: careful process reduces mistakes, but too much segmentation can slow the felt momentum of writing. I need to get better at preserving the benefits of structure without making the process feel fragmented.

## Lessons Learned
- For CRDB-style Thai report writing, dense synthesis sections should often end with compact summary tables
- Sponsor-facing Thai prose should prefer operational examples over generic institutional language when discussing constraints
- CDM explanation in main chapters must follow actual subject areas and sub-domains, not just general architectural statements
- Full sitemap detail is better moved to appendices while the body focuses on logic and implications
- When a file is clearly the anchor, supporting mappings should often be embedded there instead of split out separately
