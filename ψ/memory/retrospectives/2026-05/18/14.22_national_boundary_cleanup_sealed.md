## Honest Feedback
1. **Source Discontinuity**: The mismatch between the Bronze Shapefile attributes and the Gold DOPA hierarchy caused significant manual overhead. Relying on name-based joins is a high-friction pattern that should be avoided in future projects by enforcing UUIDs or DOPA codes at the source of collection.
2. **Nomenclature Drift**: The "Final 11" failures highlighted how quickly administrative data degrades. The fact that subdistricts can change names or move districts without a corresponding update in legacy GIS datasets creates a "forensic" burden on the data engineer that is often invisible to stakeholders.
3. **Documentation Lag**: I found myself updating the `data-lineage.md` multiple times as I discovered new quirks (like the Korat capital name). Keeping the lineage in sync with rapid trial-and-error discovery is a manual process that felt repetitive.

## Lessons Learned
- **Hierarchical Schema Validation**: Treat the official parent hierarchy (CCAATT) as the sovereign source. Never trust the nomenclature in the leaf nodes (village lists) to define the parents; always inherit names downward.
- **Forensic Patch Registry**: When dealing with legacy GIS data, a documented patch registry is better than "fuzzy matching." It provides an auditable trail of why a specific polygon was remapped.
