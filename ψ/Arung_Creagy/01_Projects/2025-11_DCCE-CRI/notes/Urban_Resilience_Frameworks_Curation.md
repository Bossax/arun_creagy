## Timeline: what “urban resilience” evolved into (high-level)

From the extracted timeline:

- **Pre-2000s → early 2000s**: engineering robustness vs ecological absorption/adaptation framing (e.g., “origins of the term ‘resilience’ imply strength and resistance…” (Tyler & Moench, 2012) captured in [`Chronological Timeline of Urban Resilience.md`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Chronological%20Timeline%20of%20Urban%20Resilience.md:20)).
- **2009–2012**: pivot to **governance, agents, SES** (e.g., governance dimensions in Tanner et al. 2009; “systems, agents, institutions” in Tyler & Moench 2012) (see [`Chronological Timeline of Urban Resilience.md`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Chronological%20Timeline%20of%20Urban%20Resilience.md:32)).
- **2015–2018**: explicit **framing divisions** (system vs community) + more systematized dimensions (see [`Chronological Timeline of Urban Resilience.md`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Chronological%20Timeline%20of%20Urban%20Resilience.md:40)).
- **2020s**: “systemic readiness”, composite indices, and (in some frameworks) high-velocity data fusion (digital twins/IoT) (see [`Chronological Timeline of Urban Resilience.md`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Chronological%20Timeline%20of%20Urban%20Resilience.md:49)).

**Curation implication:** CRI Phase 2 can legitimately **prefer governance/process/capabilities** over static “asset existence” checks, because this is a recognized evolution in the field.

## Curated framework profiles (short)

### 1) Good Urban Governance for Climate Change Adaptation (Tanner et al., 2009)

- **Evidence of real-city application:** rapid assessment across 10 Asian cities, as captured in [`Classification of Urban Resilience Frameworks.md`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Classification%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:21).
- **Core interpretation:** governance is the primary driver; five governance dimensions are the measurement lens (see [`Short-listed Frameworks for CRI Phase 2.md`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Short-listed%20Frameworks%20for%20CRI%20Phase%202.md:19)).
- **Data method:** rapid qualitative diagnostics (primary+secondary), with a known risk of positive reporting bias in consultant-led assessments (see [`Data Requirements and Practicality...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Data%20Requirements%20and%20Practicality%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:22)).
- **Use pattern:** a governance diagnosis that can inform where “process” reforms are needed.
- **Implication for CRI:** can be reused as a **governance rubric** (dimensions) even if the full primary-data method is not feasible.

### 2) Urban Climate Resilience Framework / ACCCRN (Tyler & Moench, 2012)

- **Evidence of real-city application:** planning activities in 10 Asian cities (see [`Classification...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Classification%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:13)).
- **Core structure:** systems + social agents + institutions; agent capacities such as responsiveness/resourcefulness/learning (see [`Short-listed...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Short-listed%20Frameworks%20for%20CRI%20Phase%202.md:15)).
- **Data method:** participatory Shared Learning Dialogues (see [`Implementation and Use...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Implementation%20and%20Use%20of%20Applied%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:15)).
- **Use pattern:** generates city-specific vulnerability assessment + resilience strategies (see [`Implementation and Use...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Implementation%20and%20Use%20of%20Applied%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:18)).
- **Implication for CRI:** best used as **framing + interpretation**; not as a strict “secondary-data only” index recipe.

### 3) Strategy& Urban Resilience Assessment Framework

- **Evidence of real-city application:** 9 MENA cities + comparators (see [`Classification...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Classification%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:29)).
- **Core structure:** vulnerabilities across domains + capacity to Respond/Recover/Transform (see [`Data Requirements and Practicality...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Data%20Requirements%20and%20Practicality%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:27)).
- **Data method:** secondary data KPIs (131), normalized; may rely on national proxies (see [`Data Requirements and Practicality...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Data%20Requirements%20and%20Practicality%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:32)).
- **Implication for CRI:** a direct pattern match for “capacity buckets” + “mostly secondary data” + “profiling”.

### 4) Global Cities Resilience Index (GCRI; Kearney, 2025)

- **Evidence of real-city application:** 31 global cities analyzed (see [`Classification...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Classification%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:45)).
- **Core contribution to feasibility:** “quantifiable, verifiable data” + explicit “data richness” weighting (see [`Data Requirements and Practicality...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Data%20Requirements%20and%20Practicality%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:66)).
- **Use pattern:** designed to avoid fixed rankings; track over time (see [`Implementation and Use...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Implementation%20and%20Use%20of%20Applied%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:52)).
- **Implication for CRI:** strongly supports a provincial index where missingness is structural; “data richness” becomes a defensible adjustment.

### 5) ICARIA RAF (Brito et al., 2026)

- **Evidence of real-city application:** AMB Spain (plus exploratory SLR) (see [`Classification...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Classification%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:53)).
- **Core output pattern:** multidimensional reporting; categorical development levels; explicitly not a single global indicator (see [`Implementation and Use...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Implementation%20and%20Use%20of%20Applied%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:58)).
- **Known barrier:** data fragmentation across stakeholders (see [`Short-listed...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Short-listed%20Frameworks%20for%20CRI%20Phase%202.md:58)).
- **Implication for CRI:** adopt the **communication pattern** (incipient/progressing/advanced) and assume partial data.

### 6) RESOLUTE / ERMG (Bellini et al., 2022)

- **Evidence of application:** Florence + Athens Metro pilots (see [`Data Requirements and Practicality...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Data%20Requirements%20and%20Practicality%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:40)).
- **Data regime:** big data + real-time sensors + social + GPS (see [`Data Requirements and Practicality...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Data%20Requirements%20and%20Practicality%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:43)).
- **Implication for CRI:** not implementable under current constraints, but provides a “north star” for future platform evolution.

### 7) IDIS (Martínez-Fernández et al., 2022)

- **Evidence of application:** multiple cases including Fuerteventura (see [`Implementation and Use...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Implementation%20and%20Use%20of%20Applied%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:28)).
- **Core pattern:** avoid mixed indices; use thresholds to reject unsafe options (see [`Implementation and Use...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Implementation%20and%20Use%20of%20Applied%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:26)).
- **Implication for CRI:** incorporate “threshold / red-flag” rules alongside profiles (communication and defensibility).

## Practical takeaways for CRI (from the curated set)

1) **Governance is consistently central** in applied frameworks and often treated as a core pillar or multiplier (see governance weighting discussion in [`Data Requirements and Practicality...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Data%20Requirements%20and%20Practicality%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:126)).

2) **“Secondary-data-only” patterns exist** (Strategy&, GCRI) and can be adapted directly (see compatibility analysis in [`Frameworks Compatible...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Frameworks%20Compatible%20with%20Capacity-Based,%20Secondary-Data,%20and%20Profiling%20Constraints.md:40)).

3) **Avoid the ranking trap** by borrowing:
- categorical development levels (ICARIA) (see [`Short-listed...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Short-listed%20Frameworks%20for%20CRI%20Phase%202.md:42))
- dynamic tracking vs fixed ranks (GCRI) (see [`Implementation and Use...`](src/01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Implementation%20and%20Use%20of%20Applied%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md:96))

1) **Explicitly design for data poverty** via “data richness” weighting (GCRI) (see [`Data Requirements and Practicality...`]( 01_Projects/2025-11_DCCE-CRI/notes/Data%20Requirements%20and%20Practicality%20of%20Urban%20Resilience%20Frameworks.md )).
