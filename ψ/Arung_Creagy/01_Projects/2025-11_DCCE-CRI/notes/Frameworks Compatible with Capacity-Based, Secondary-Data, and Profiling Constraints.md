### **(a) Extraction: Frameworks Compatible with Capacity-Based, Secondary-Data, and Profiling Constraints**

**1. Strategy& Urban Resilience Assessment Framework**

- **Chronological Context:** Post-2020 (n.d.). Developed as a direct reaction to the COVID-19 pandemic and rising climate/cyber hazards to shift urban measurement from static vulnerabilities to dynamic institutional capacities (Strategy&, n.d.).
- **Capacity-Based Approach:** Explicitly structures its entire assessment around three core capacities: “CAPACITY TO RESPOND... CAPACITY TO RECOVER... CAPACITY TO TRANSFORM: The ability to advance economically, socially, and technologically with new systems, structures, and reconfigurations through innovation” (Strategy&, n.d.).
- **Data Requirements:** Relies exclusively on secondary administrative and statistical data without primary surveying, utilizing a composite index based on "131 key performance indicators (KPIs)" sourced from "World Bank, World Development Indicators, Worldwide Governance Indicators... UN Food and Agriculture Organization... national statistics" (Strategy&, n.d.).
- **Profiling over Ranking:** Instead of just scoring cities, the framework generates cluster mappings ("Limited", "Moderate", "Advanced" capacity) to help cities design tailored "resilience blueprints" (Strategy&, n.d.).
- **Evidence of Real City Application:** “In the MENA region we applied our urban resilience framework to Abu Dhabi, Amman, Cairo, Casablanca, Dubai, Jeddah, Kuwait City, Muscat, and Riyadh” (Strategy&, n.d.).
- **Implication for downstream analysis:** This framework provides the exact architectural template for mapping existing administrative data directly into Respond/Recover/Transform capability buckets.

**2. Global Cities Resilience Index (GCRI)**

- **Chronological Context:** 2025. Created by Kearney and FII in reaction to traditional ESG indices that measure "past achievements", shifting the focus to "systemic readiness" for future uncertainties (Kearney, 2025).
- **Data Requirements:** Perfectly matches the constraint to avoid primary data collection. It relies entirely on "quantifiable, verifiable data from established sources rather than survey responses, expert opinions... or subjective evaluations" (Kearney, 2025).
- **Profiling over Ranking:** Specifically rejects the "ranking trap" to support capacity-building: "The framework explicitly avoids creating fixed rankings that will become outdated" and instead provides visual "resilience profiles" designed as a "diagnostic framework for identifying systemic gaps" (Kearney, 2025).
- **SES/Management Grounding:** Evaluates readiness across interconnected socio-economic dimensions like "institutional governance; sustainable finance and business; technology and innovation; social and human capital; and global integration" (Kearney, 2025).
- **Evidence of Real City Application:** “Our analysis of 31 cities for the first edition of the GCRI—across Global North and Global South economies—provides insights into the foundations that enable urban resilience” (Kearney, 2025).
- **Implication for downstream analysis:** The GCRI's methodology of mathematically weighting administrative data by "data richness" offers a critical statistical technique for building capacity profiles when secondary data quality varies.

**3. ICARIA Holistic Resilience Assessment Framework (RAF)**

- **Chronological Context:** 2026. Upgrades the earlier RESCCUE framework to expand focus onto regional ecosystems and nature-based solutions, emphasizing interconnected socio-ecological services (Brito et al., 2026).
- **Capacity-Based Approach:** Focuses its critical infrastructure and services evaluations around continuous “resilience capabilities (withstand–recover–adapt–anticipate–prepare)” (Brito et al., 2026).
- **Data Requirements:** Utilizes existing secondary data synthesized from administrative "models, plans, monitoring activities, risk assessment, or city incident records, from various platforms and documents" (Brito et al., 2026).
- **Profiling over Ranking:** Explicitly states it is “not intended to produce a single global resilience indicator. Instead, it supports multidimensional decision-making” by reporting the percentage of metrics achieved at categorical development levels (incipient, progressing, advanced) (Brito et al., 2026).
- **Evidence of Real City Application:** “Overall results of a Spanish metropolitan area (AMB) and an exploratory application to an Austrian rural case (SLR) are also presented” (Brito et al., 2026).
- **Implication for downstream analysis:** Adopting ICARIA's categorical output visualizations (e.g., incipient vs. advanced capacity profiles) is an effective design choice to translate raw statistical data into policy-relevant narratives for municipal departments.

### **(b) Synthesis: Compatibility Analysis**

Based on the specified design constraints, the **Strategy& Urban Resilience Assessment Framework** and the **Global Cities Resilience Index (GCRI)** are the most highly compatible frameworks in the literature, with the **ICARIA RAF** serving as a strong supplementary model.

- **Aligning with the Capacity and SES/Management Focus:** The Strategy& framework explicitly uses the exact "Respond, Recover, Transform" capacity terminology requested. It is heavily grounded in management science, focusing on how institutional governance, sustainable finance, and delivery of public services (the management elements) mitigate vulnerabilities across basic needs, society, economy, and the urban environment (the SES elements). The ICARIA framework supplements this by explicitly measuring capabilities to "withstand–recover–adapt–anticipate–prepare" while heavily integrating natural areas and ecosystem services into its administrative assessment.
- **Aligning with the Administrative Data Constraint:** Both the Strategy& framework and the GCRI strictly forbid the use of primary surveys or subjective expert scoring. They are engineered to ingest existing "quantifiable, verifiable data" from national databases, the World Bank, WHO, and municipal incident records. By tagging these existing data points into capacity buckets (e.g., tagging the existence of a diversification strategy under "Capacity to Recover"), they eliminate the need for costly primary data collection.
- **Aligning with the Profiling Goal (Avoiding the Ranking Trap):** The GCRI and ICARIA frameworks are explicitly designed to avoid single, reductionist "league table" scores. The GCRI states it "avoids creating fixed rankings" to instead generate dimensional "resilience profiles" (radar charts of readiness), while ICARIA outputs the percentage of a city's services operating at "incipient, progressing, or advanced" levels. This ensures the output serves as a diagnostic gap-analysis tool for policy intervention rather than a superficial competition.
