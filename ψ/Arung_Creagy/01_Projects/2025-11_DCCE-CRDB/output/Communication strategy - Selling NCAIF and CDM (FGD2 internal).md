# Communication strategy — FGD2 internal buy-in (NCAIF + CDM)

## Purpose

Secure **active engagement** (not passive attendance) from internal DCCE stakeholders for FGD2 by:

1. Creating continuity with FGD1 (“you said → we did”).
2. Making the **ask** concrete: a small set of **decisions** to unblock Phase 1 delivery.
3. Reducing cognitive load (plain language, minimal jargon, 3-depth explanation).

## Audience segmentation (internal)

### A) Decision owners (Directors / heads of divisions)

**What they care about**

- Risk of public-facing embarrassment (April public hearing)
- Clear ownership/accountability
- Budget/operational feasibility

**What we need from them**

- Confirm decision ownership and sign-off model for publishing, glossary authority, QA/QC

### B) Domain owners (Adaptation / Risk / M&E / GHG focal points)

**What they care about**

- Correctness of definitions and indicators
- Not being forced into unrealistic data expectations (timeliness, granularity)

**What we need from them**

- Agree on Phase 1 scope of flagship products and minimum quality/metadata thresholds

### C) Digital / data platform team

**What they care about**

- Implementation complexity and ongoing maintenance
- Data integration constraints and boundary crosswalk feasibility

**What we need from them**

- Confirm “Phase 1 feasible” constraints and minimum viable workflow (preview, publishing states)

### D) PR / communications

**What they care about**

- Publishing safety (review-before-public)
- Plain-language narrative + usable content formats

**What we need from them**

- Agree on draft/review/publish flow, messaging tiering (policy/practitioners/public), and FAQs/feedback routing

## Core message stack (what we repeat)

1. **Continuity:** “We used FGD1 to redesign how we present and govern the information.”
2. **Scope:** “Phase 1 is not everything; it is the minimum that is safe and useful.”
3. **Safety:** “Publishing requires a workflow and accountability (PR + domain + governance).”
4. **Usability:** “Three depths: policy / practitioners / public.”
5. **Practicality:** “We explicitly label timeliness, uncertainty, and limitations.”

## Pre-read pack (what to send)

### 1) One-page executive brief (T-3 days)

**Content (1 page max):**

- What is FGD2 (goal + why now)
- 5–7 bullets: “FGD1 pain points → design response”
- The decision list (D1–D11) as short yes/no or choose-1 prompts

## Channel plan (how we deliver)

Minimum viable channels:

- Official email/memo with the 1-page executive brief attached
- Calendar invite with agenda + decisions list in the description
- Internal chat/group message (short: 3 bullets + “please read 1 page before meeting”)

## Timeline (relative to meeting day)

- **T-10 to T-7:** confirm attendee list + decision owners (who must be present for which decisions)
- **T-7:** send “save the date” + 3-sentence framing (continuity + decisions + deliverables)
- **T-3:** send pre-read pack (1-page brief + decision sheet + 2-page use-case handout)
- **T-1:** reminder message (time + what to bring)
- **T+1 day:** circulate decision log and named owners

## What to ask attendees to bring (low-effort, high-signal)

Ask each attendee to bring **one concrete example** related to their work:

- A dataset they manage (or want) and why it matters
- A recent request they received (“can you give me X map/table?”)
- One publishing fear (what would be unsafe to publish without review)

This makes the session grounded without requiring technical knowledge.

## Success criteria

The comms strategy succeeded if by end of FGD2 we have:

- Named owners for publishing workflow + glossary authority + QA/QC checkpoints
- Agreed Phase 1 product shortlist + export requirements
- Agreed canonical boundary stance + crosswalk ownership
- A decision log that can be emailed without re-explaining context
