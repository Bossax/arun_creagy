### 1. Role and Responsibility of the Agency

- **Key duty and roles:** OTP acts as the primary agency responsible for developing transport and traffic policies and plans. In the context of climate adaptation, they plan to shift their focus specifically toward policy formulation and evaluation after establishing initial adaptation guidelines for implementing agencies.
    
    
- **Associated legal frameworks that institutionalize the roles:** While specific legal acts were not explicitly cited in the interview, OTP is institutionalized as the central transport planning authority for the Ministry of Transport, holding jurisdiction over national-level infrastructure planning.

### 3. Current Workflow & Data Usage

- **Context:** The transport sector is not currently included in the national National Adaptation Plan (NAP). OTP proactively initiated its own adaptation pilot project rather than waiting for national inclusion.
    
    +4
    
- **Hazard Scope:** The project evaluates immediate risks from floods, earthquakes, and landslides. Sea-level rise is factored in specifically for ports. Tropical storms are currently excluded.
    
    +3
    
- **Data Sources:** They utilize 10-year historical water data, GIS risk points, and 50-to-100-year rainfall models.
    
    +2
    
- **Modeling Techniques and Granularity:** Standard national hazard maps lack the required resolution for infrastructure planning. OTP hired consultants to build custom, high-resolution hydrological models to map hazard flows exactly onto transport routes. The granularity is extremely high; the models pinpoint vulnerabilities down to specific highway kilometer markers and specific utility pole numbers along railways.
    
    +4
    
- **Inter-agency Collaborations and Data Sharing:** OTP coordinates with the Royal Irrigation Department, Thai Meteorological Department (TMD), and water resource agencies (ONWR) for base data. The Department of Highways and the Department of Rural Roads assist directly with landslide field surveys.
    
    +3
    
- **Cascading Impacts:** OTP recognizes that infrastructure failures cause severe socio-economic bottlenecks. They cited recent floods in Sukhothai, Chiang Mai, Chiang Rai, and Hat Yai where transport was completely severed, requiring boats for travel and isolating communities.
    
    +3
    

### 4. Climate Data Needs & Gaps

- **Gaps in Projections:** OTP urgently requires DCCE to provide authoritative, highly accepted long-term climate forecast data—especially hydrological projections—that can be utilized annually across all ministries.
    
    +1
    
- **Gaps in Baseline Indicators:** Because the transport sector is absent from the NAP, OTP lacks predefined national indicators for evaluating adaptation risks and had to establish their own baseline frameworks.
    
    +1
    
- **Budgeting and Policy Roadblocks:** Implementing climate adaptation is severely stymied by the Bureau of the Budget. While engineering departments routinely account for standard flood and earthquake resilience, factoring in 50-to-100-year climate uncertainties drastically inflates construction costs (e.g., up to 50 times). Budget requests for long-term adaptation are almost universally rejected due to the perceived uncertainty of the threat.
    
    +4
    
- **Requests for DCCE Intervention:** OTP specifically requests that DCCE assist in communicating the necessity of worst-case scenario planning to budget allocators to establish a shared understanding. They also recommend studying international frameworks, citing Australia (Queensland) as a model where dedicated, centralized disaster management funds successfully bypass standard rigid budget constraints.
    
    +4
    

### 5. Next Steps & CRDB Integration

- **Map to Implementation Plan:** DCCE is currently partnering with Ramkhamhaeng University to finalize a downscaled climate model for Thailand (10-20 sq km resolution) by mid-year; this directly addresses OTP's urgent need for centralized projection data and will be a core asset in the CRDB ecosystem.
    
    +1
    
- **Seed Use Cases (WP3/WP4):** OTP’s high-resolution flood modeling is an advanced, real-world "seed use case" for the Conceptual Data Model (CDM) outlined in WP3, perfectly illustrating the complex spatial relationship between "Exposed Assets" (poles, km markers) and "Hazard Events". Furthermore, the datasets within OTP's upcoming Open Data Center and their pilot project outputs must be registered in the WP4 Data Catalog.
    
    +4
    
- **Planned Participation in Workshops:** OTP will be formally invited to the CRDB public hearing and brainstorming workshop in April 2026. They will participate as a "co-producer" of sectoral impact data, networking with upstream earth observation data providers like GISTDA and TMD to refine the proposed Data Architecture.
    
    +2
