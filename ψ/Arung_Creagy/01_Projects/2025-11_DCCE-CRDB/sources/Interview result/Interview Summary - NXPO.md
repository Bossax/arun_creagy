**Interviewee Name/Title:** Dr. Fang (Director of Sustainability Division)

**Department:** Sustainability Division

**Agency:** Office of National Higher Education Science Research and Innovation Policy Council (NXPO / สอวช.)

### 1. Role and Responsibility of the Agency

- **Key duty and roles:** NXPO serves as a national policy-making and strategic planning body under the Ministry of Higher Education, Science, Research and Innovation (MHESI). The Sustainability Division acts as a system integrator, focusing on "system change" by using innovation, research funding, and human resource development to break down governmental silos. They formulate cross-cutting policies for climate change (both mitigation and adaptation) and act as the National Designated Entity (NDE) for technology mechanisms in international climate negotiations.
    
- **Associated legal frameworks:** NXPO drives policy through national boards, such as the National Higher Education, Science, Research and Innovation Policy Council (chaired by the Prime Minister). They recently submitted the "MHESI for Climate Change" agenda to this council.
    

### 2. Highlighted Key Projects

- **Ministry of Water (Proposed) / MHESI for Water:**
    
    - _Status & Scope:_ Policy formulation/Advocacy. NXPO led the structural design for a proposed Ministry of Water, aiming to consolidate the 52 existing water-related agencies. They are pushing to establish an "Intelligent Unit" as a quick win to standardize and verify water data across the country.
        
    - _Roadblocks:_ Severe institutional fragmentation; 28% of water is controlled by the Royal Irrigation Department under its own laws, and groundwater is controlled by the Department of Groundwater Resources. Existing overarching water laws (e.g., Water Act of 2017) are often overridden by older, agency-specific legislation.
        
- **Research Framework (2027-2032) with TSRI (สกสว.):**
    
    - _Status & Scope:_ Active. A 5-year funding framework utilizing the Science, Research and Innovation (SRI) Fund. It features three pillars: Mitigation, Adaptation, and Systems Research. It also focuses on developing "Green Skills" for government and private sectors.
        
- **Net Zero Campus / Climate Innovation Sandbox:**
    
    - _Status & Scope:_ Active. Using 172 universities as testing grounds (sandboxes) for climate innovation and adaptation. The goal is to turn universities into local "expert hubs" to assist local authorities (LAOs) with resilience planning, as LAOs typically lack academic capacity.
        
- **AI Platform for Research Data Integration (with TSRI):**
    
    - _Status & Scope:_ Recently Funded. NXPO helped TSRI procure an AI system to ingest billions of baht worth of historical research reports. The AI will analyze this unstructured data to identify knowledge gaps, which will then inform DCCE on where to direct future climate research funding.
        

### 3. Current Workflow & Data Usage

- **Context, Hazard Scope, and Data Sources:** When formulating national policy via "Double Diamond" design thinking, NXPO heavily relies on macro-level data. They currently scrape data from foreign reports, DCCE presentation slides, and consulting firm outputs (like CREATIVE's).
    
- **Modeling Techniques and Granularity:** They utilize Foresight methodologies to create scenarios. However, the interviewee noted a severe mismatch between data supply and demand: developers build Hydro-Informatics models without knowing what data end-users actually need to make decisions, resulting in massive data collection (e.g., by the National Statistical Office) that goes entirely unused.
    
- **Inter-agency Collaborations & Cascading Impacts:** NXPO recognizes that adaptation is currently too hyper-focused on water (flooding/drought). They are strongly advocating for the inclusion of cascading impacts like heatwaves, health crises (disease vectors), and food degradation into the national adaptation dialogue.
    

### 4. Climate Data Needs & Gaps

- **Projections, Baselines, and Standardizations:** * _The "Verification" Gap:_ There is no standardized, unified baseline for climate data. Agencies collect the same data points but generate different numbers. No single agency has the authority—or the courage—to verify and declare a "single source of truth" for national modeling.
    
    - _Resilience/Capacity Data:_ Current risk models focus entirely on hazard exposure (the "harmful level") but completely ignore the "carrying capacity" or resilience of local communities. There is an urgent need to map institutional and community resilience to understand true vulnerability.
        
    - _Assessment Reports:_ NXPO strongly recommended that DCCE revive the comprehensive "Thailand Assessment Reports" (similar to IPCC reports) previously funded by the Thailand Research Fund (TRF/สกว.), as these provide the foundational macro-data necessary for policy formulation.
        
- **Severe Policy & Institutional Roadblocks:**
    
    - _The "Culture of Accountability" (ระบบรับผิดรับชอบ):_ Government officials are terrified to share or officially publish predictive data (e.g., flood forecasts). If a forecast is wrong, the agency or individual faces severe legal and social backlash. This fear paralyzes data sharing. NXPO suggests implementing automated, locked IT systems to remove personal liability from data publishing.
        
    - _Institutional Misalignment:_ Climate change requires cross-economic, systemic management, but DCCE is housed under the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment (MNRE), limiting its economic leverage compared to models in other countries.
        
    - _Budget Justification for Uncertainty:_ Operational agencies struggle to justify budgets for climate adaptation infrastructure because the scenarios are probabilistic. If they build for a "worst-case" scenario that doesn't happen, they risk being sued for wasting state funds.
        
- **Specific Requests for DCCE:** NXPO specifically endorsed the "Climate-ADAPT (EU)" model for the CRDB platform. Given the institutional roadblocks, they believe a "Clearinghouse" or "Shopping Mall" model—where DCCE links to external data rather than owning it all—is the most viable path for Thailand.
    

### 5. Next Steps & CRDB Integration

- **Map to Implementation Plan:** NXPO's insights on institutional paralysis and the fear of data sharing perfectly validate the need for the Data Governance Framework (Risk Database Charter) in WP3. Clear Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and "Data Owner" vs. "Data Custodian" definitions are required to legally protect operational staff who share data.
    
- **Seed Use Cases for WP3 Conceptual Data Model (CDM):** * _Scenario:_ "Integrating an Institutional Resilience Index (assessing local government capacity) with localized heatwave projections to determine which municipalities require emergency university-led intervention (Net Zero Campus) during extreme heat events."
    
- **Inputs for WP4 Data Catalog:** The upcoming outputs from the TSRI/NXPO AI Research Analysis platform should be tracked as a future External System Asset, as it will highlight critical gaps in Thailand's climate research data.
