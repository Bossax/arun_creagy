# GF — way forward (freedom vs balance)

## Context (what’s actually happening)
Two true needs are colliding:
- **My need:** sovereignty (time, place, work aligned with the ocean + meaningful project).
- **Her need:** presence and stability (not living with a partner who is always “elsewhere”).

The goal is not to “win.” The goal is to **make the plan legible, bounded, and humane**—so she can consent to the season we’re entering (or we can learn that our visions are incompatible).

---

## 1) My responsibilities (non-negotiable)

### A) Turn the grind into a bounded project
If I can’t describe the grind with clear boundaries, it becomes indefinite—and that’s what makes it scary.

Write down:
- **Runway target**: savings amount + what it covers (months, rent, emergencies).
- **Start date**: when the “push phase” began.
- **End date / decision date**: the date I will reassess (not “someday”).
- **Milestones (3–5)** that prove I’m not just busy, but moving.

### B) Protect “relationship time” as a system, not a promise
Promises fail under stress; systems survive.

Pick:
- **1 weekly work-free block** (e.g., Fri 19:00–23:00) — no laptop, no planning.
- **1 shared ritual** (walk, meal, gym, tea, short trip) that happens even during busy weeks.
- **1 monthly check-in** to review: (1) how she feels, (2) how the plan is going, (3) what needs adjustment.

### C) Stop hiding the reality of the plan
Share the actual numbers and risk, not only the dream.
- If I’m behind, say it.
- If I’m scared, say it.
- If the runway plan changes, explain why.

---

## 2) What I should ask from her
Not “support me no matter what,” but specific asks:
- **Tell me early** when she feels lonely or deprioritized.
- **Help enforce the protected time** (she can call it out when I break it).
- **Be honest** if her vision for life cannot include a high-intensity season.

---

## 3) A conversation script (direct, respectful)

> “I want to talk about what I’m building and what it costs.
>
> I’m working hard because I’m trying to buy something specific: the ability to choose my time and live closer to the ocean, doing work I actually care about.
>
> I also see the cost on your side: it can feel like I’m not fully here. I don’t want you to feel like you’re waiting for a future version of me while the present disappears.
>
> So here’s the plan in concrete terms: I’m aiming for [runway target] by [date]. By [decision date], I will reassess whether the project is viable and what the next step is.
>
> And I want us to protect time that belongs to us, even during this push phase: [weekly work-free block] and [shared ritual].
>
> I’m not asking you to agree with my dream blindly. I’m asking you to look at the map with me—and to tell me honestly what you need so you don’t feel abandoned while I build it.”

---

## 4) What “agreement” looks like
Agreement means:
- She understands the timeline and milestones.
- She has real protected time with me.
- We both know what would trigger a renegotiation (missed milestones, burnout, emotional distance).

---

## 5) Red flags (in me)
- I keep extending the timeline without updating the plan.
- I treat her needs as “distractions” instead of legitimate costs.
- I cancel every shared block “just this once” for weeks.

When these happen, I’m not becoming freer—I’m becoming narrower.

---

## 6) Hard fork (if needed)
If, after making the plan bounded and protecting shared time, she still cannot accept any intense season, then the question becomes compatibility:
- Can we design a life that holds both sovereignty and presence?
- If not, it’s better to learn that early, gently, and honestly.
