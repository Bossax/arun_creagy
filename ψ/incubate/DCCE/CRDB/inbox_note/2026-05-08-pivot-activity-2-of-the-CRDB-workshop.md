Director Toey does not by the concept of MVPs. He sees them as unclear and difficult to understand. He wants us to present the whole list of use cases and needs that we extract from interviews.

So the activity 2 will be reframed. We strip off the MVP facade of those original use cases and regroup them based on broad themes of how information is needed for a specific objective. 

Below is a rough concept of  how activity 2 will be run.

---
The pattern  **Read -> Vote -> Propose -> Discuss -> Distill** is a very safe and effective approach. It moves the participants from "passive listeners" to "active critics" and finally "contributors."

Here is a structured design for **Activity 2 (1 Hour)** that follows your logic and avoids group dependency:


### **Activity 2: The "Needs & Services" Marketplace (60 Minutes)**

#### **1. Browsing the "Menu" (10 Mins)**

Instead of a long document, give each table a **"Needs Menu"** (a one-page sheet or large cards) showing the 4 Problem Spaces we identified earlier:

1. **Economic/Financial** (Loss estimation, Budgeting)
    
2. **Operational/Area-Based** (Local risk maps, Real-time data)
    
3. **Social/Equity** (Vulnerable groups, Local capacity)
    
4. **Foundation** (Standards, Clearing House, Metadata)
    

**The Instruction:** "Read through these needs captured from the first 10 agencies. Do you see yourself here? Use your **Green Stickers** to vote for the 2 most 'Urgent' needs for your agency's mission."

#### **2. Proposing the "Missing Details" (15 Mins)**

Director Toey wants detail, so we ask the participants to add what the 10+ interviews missed.

**The Instruction:** "What is the **ONE specific situation** at your work that isn't covered by this menu? Write it on a 'Detail Card'."

- _Detail Card Template:_ **"To do [Mission/Job], I need [Specific Data] from [Agency Name], but currently [The Barrier]."**
    

#### **3. The Mixed-Role "Stress Test" (20 Mins)**

This is where the mixed-role grouping shines. The participants discuss the "Detail Cards" they just wrote.

- **The User** explains their card.
    
- **The Producer** (at the same table) says: _"I have that data, but it's in a format you might not like"_ or _"We don't collect that yet."_
    
- **The Funder/NESDC** (at the same table) says: _"If you two had this, I would approve the budget for the project."_
    

**Facilitator Role:** No tech skills needed. Just walk around and ask: _"Is this 'Detail Card' a shared problem for others at this table?"_

#### **4. Distilling the Lesson (15 Mins)**

Each group identifies the **"Most Valuable Service"** that emerged from their discussion—something that would solve the friction they just debated.

**The Instruction:** "If DCCE were to build a service to solve your table’s biggest 'Detail Card,' what would that service be called and who would it serve?"

---

### **Why this avoids the Pitfalls:**

1. **Not Group-Dependent:** Whether you have BMA or Agency X doesn't matter. Any "User" can explain a "Mission" and any "Producer" can explain a "Data Barrier." The _logic_ remains the same.
    
2. **Solves the "Abstract MVP" Problem:** Participants aren't looking at a "Clearing House" (abstract). They are looking at their own **"Detail Cards"** (concrete).
    
3. **Safe for Facilitators:** The facilitators aren't teaching; they are managing a "Marketplace" of ideas. They are just ensuring the cards are filled out.
    
4. **Learning & Distillation:** By voting on the "Menu" first, they learn what other 10 agencies care about. By discussing their own cards, they distill the common barriers that DCCE needs to hear.
    

**The Output for you:** By the end of the hour, you will have:

1. **Quantitative Data:** Voting patterns on the 4 Problem Spaces.
    
2. **Qualitative Detail:** 30+ new "Detail Cards" explaining the specific "Data -> Mission -> Barrier" links.
    
3. **Prioritized Services:** 6 "Most Valuable Service" concepts distilled by the groups.
