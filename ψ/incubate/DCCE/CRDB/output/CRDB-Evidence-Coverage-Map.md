- which gap lenses already have usable support,
- where the evidence is strongest,
- what remains weak or under-specified, and
- what should be gathered next for meeting and workshop preparation.

## Methodological routing of evidence streams

To avoid conflating how different evidence streams were used, the CRDB work routes them through four distinct methodological steps:

- **Literature and standards → CDM core**
  - IPCC and WMO guidance, ISO-style data and quality standards, conceptual modeling notes, and comparative architecture analysis are used to justify and shape the CDM skeleton in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/Conceptual Data Model for climate risk and adaptation data system.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/Conceptual%20Data%20Model%20for%20climate%20risk%20and%20adaptation%20data%20system.md) and related synthesis in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-12_crdb-section1-cdm-mvp-evidence-analysis.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-12_crdb-section1-cdm-mvp-evidence-analysis.md).
- **Interviews and FGDs → use cases and needs clusters**
  - Stakeholder interviews and FGD evidence are translated into use cases and demand clusters in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/NCAIF_Use_Cases.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/NCAIF_Use_Cases.md), [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-23-Chapter2-stakeholder-needs-synthesis-v2.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-23-Chapter2-stakeholder-needs-synthesis-v2.md), and [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-23-Chapter2-interview-comparison-matrix-v2.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-23-Chapter2-interview-comparison-matrix-v2.md), with traceability preserved in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-23-CRDB-Interview-Ingestion-Traceability-Note.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-23-CRDB-Interview-Ingestion-Traceability-Note.md).
- **Use cases + UX + predecessor platforms → NCAIF sitemap and MVPs**
  - The NCAIF information architecture and MVP choices are derived from the use-case and needs synthesis above, together with UX evidence in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/inbox_source/2026-03-12 - User Experience Design Principles for National Climate Change Adaptation Information Services.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/inbox_source/2026-03-12%20-%20User%20Experience%20Design%20Principles%20for%20National%20Climate%20Change%20Adaptation%20Information%20Services.md), current DCCE website evidence such as [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-12-DCCE_Website_Content_Gap_Inventory.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-12-DCCE_Website_Content_Gap_Inventory.md), and the three-platform/T-PLAT review in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-13-Three-Platform Review and T-PLAT Lessons.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-13-Three-Platform Review and T-PLAT Lessons.md).
- **Execution gaps + sponsor realities → governance and operating model**
  - Execution-gap analysis and sponsor interactions, especially [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/inbox_note/2026-03-23-general-execution-gaps-for-NCAIF-CDM-and-data-governance.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-23-general-execution-gaps-for-NCAIF-CDM-and-data-governance.md) and locked decisions in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/phase1_decision_log.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/archive/phase1_decision_log.md), drive the design of the data-governance and operating-model approach.

## 1. Product and content gaps

### Current evidence available
- Website and portal evidence is already substantial in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-12-DCCE_Website_Content_Gap_Inventory.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-12-DCCE_Website_Content_Gap_Inventory.md), [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-12-DCCE_Website_Content_Gap_Matrix.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-12-DCCE_Website_Content_Gap_Matrix.md), and [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-12-DCCE_Website_Content_Gap_Summary.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-12-DCCE_Website_Content_Gap_Summary.md).
- The intended future-facing structure is already framed in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/National Climate Adaptation Information Framework.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/National%20Climate%20Adaptation%20Information%20Framework.md).
- User-facing navigation logic is explicitly supported by the UX evidence base in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/inbox_source/2026-03-12 - User Experience Design Principles for National Climate Change Adaptation Information Services.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/inbox_source/2026-03-12%20-%20User%20Experience%20Design%20Principles%20for%20National%20Climate%20Change%20Adaptation%20Information%20Services.md), especially for information scent, shallow navigation, matrix entry routes, progressive disclosure, and separation of tools from data catalogs.
- Stakeholder demand for tiered services, discoverability, and usable interfaces is reinforced in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-23-Chapter2-stakeholder-needs-synthesis-v2.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-23-Chapter2-stakeholder-needs-synthesis-v2.md).

### Apparent strength

**Strong.** The project already has enough evidence to justify why the current content and product surface is insufficient and what direction the NCAIF should take.

### Remaining information need
- Confirm which parts of the user-facing surface DCCE will actively maintain.
- Clarify what becomes a curated explainer, what becomes a catalog record, and what remains an external source link.
- Confirm which product changes should be shown first in the Director Toey progress discussion versus held for workshop validation.

## Priority missing information to gather next

1. **Named operating roles for priority functions** — who owns topic curation, metadata stewardship, baseline endorsement, publishing decisions, and methodology notes.
2. **Topic-specific baseline endorsement rules** — what evidence and minimum metadata are required before a dataset is treated as recommended.
3. **Boundary decisions for service design** — for each major topic, what is hosted, linked, interpreted, or kept internal.
4. **Workshop validation targets** — which stakeholders should validate baselines, access rules, granularity needs, and service tiers during the late-April workshop.
5. **Short evidence pack for management reporting** — a compact narrative for Director Toey that turns current evidence into explicit progress, unresolved issues, and next decisions.
