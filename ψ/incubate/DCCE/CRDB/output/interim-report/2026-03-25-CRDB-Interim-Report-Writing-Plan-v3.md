- Use evidence artifacts (benchmark tables, structure notes, interview tables, focus-group summaries, source inventories, matrices, selection notes, coverage-gap notes, synthesis outlines) to **fill gaps, correct inaccuracies, and satisfy feedback**, while keeping the v1 MD as the starting scaffold.
- For every TOR clause (5.2.1–5.2.5, 5.3.1–5.3.2, 5.5.1–5.5.2), follow a consistent 4-step writing workflow (below) so that the main report body becomes a concise, clause-ordered narrative and the appendices hold the detailed evidence packets.

## Generic Per‑TOR Writing Workflow (applies to 5.2.x, 5.3.x, 5.5.x)
For each TOR clause (5.2.1–5.2.5, 5.3.1–5.3.2, 5.5.1–5.5.2), apply this 4‑step workflow:

1. **Locate v1 content (MD):**
   - In the v1 MD main body and appendix, identify all paragraphs, tables, and lists that correspond to the clause.
   - Mark these excerpts (copy into a working buffer or comment them) without rewriting yet.

2. **Rearrange and trim into TOR‑ordered subsection:**
   - Create a subsection in the main body using the exact TOR heading and numbering (e.g. “5.2.1 Benchmarking of climate information platforms”).
   - Rearrange the located v1 content into a logical flow that directly answers the clause.
   - Trim repetition, drafting notes, and off‑scope detail so the subsection is concise and focused on the TOR requirement.

3. **Top up from evidence artifacts:**
   - Identify relevant evidence artifacts (benchmark tables, structure notes, FGD tables, interview coverage tables, source inventories, matrices, selection notes, coverage-gap notes, synthesis outlines).
   - Add missing facts, counts, and clarifications from these artifacts into the main-body subsection so that the clause is fully satisfied and feedback is addressed.
   - Where evidence is extensive, summarize patterns and key numbers in the main body and keep detailed rows or verbatim notes in the appendix.

4. **Push detailed material back to appendices (MD):**
   - Move or keep detailed tables, row-level evidence, long lists, and transcripts in the appendix MD file.
   - In the main body clause subsection, maintain a complete **narrative summary** that points clearly to specific appendix elements (e.g. “see Appendix A1 for full benchmark table”).
   - Ensure cross‑references are consistent and follow a simple pattern (clause → appendix table or note).
