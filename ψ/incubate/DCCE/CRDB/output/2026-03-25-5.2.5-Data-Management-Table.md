# TOR 5.2.5 – Data Management Structure Table

Tiers of the data‑management structure from **sources → CDM → NCAIF → products**, aligned with v1 main report Ch.1 §1.8–1.10 and appendices ก/ข.

| Tier / layer | Description | Ownership / stewardship | Update cadence (if specified) | Access model (rail) | v1 PDF / appendix anchor |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Source systems and datasets (raw layer) | Original datasets produced by agencies: climate and hazard data, socio‑economic baselines, disaster and loss records, adaptation plans/projects, resilience indicators. NCAIF does not re‑host all sources in Phase 1; it references them. | Line‑agency **data owners** (e.g. technical ministries, national statistics office, disaster agency) and DCCE divisions for their own products. | As defined by each producer (from near‑real‑time event reporting to multi‑year surveys); v1 notes heterogeneity rather than a single standard. | Mixed by classification: **public** datasets as open data, **inter‑agency** datasets via G2G exchange, **internal‑only** datasets kept within the originating system. | Main report: Ch.1 §1.6–1.7 (landscape of existing products); Ch.2 §2.1–2.4 (current supply, cadence, and constraints of datasets). |
| 2. CDM‑conformant logical layer (integration layer) | Logical representations of key entities and relationships using the CDM (e.g. `CLIMATE_DRIVER`, `HAZARDOUS_EVENT`, `RISK_ASSESSMENT`, `EXPOSED_ASSET`, `LOSS_DAMAGE_RECORD`, `ADAPTATION_PROJECT`, `COMPOSITE_INDEX`). This layer organises how different source datasets can be combined. | DCCE **central data / architecture team**, with input from domain experts and source‑agency counterparts for each subject area. | Updated when Phase‑1 products require new entities or relationships; otherwise stable between major design reviews. | Internal design and documentation layer; exposed indirectly via NCAIF methods/metadata pages rather than as a public database. | Main report: Ch.1 §1.8–1.9 describe CDM subject areas and central role of risk/impact; Appendix ก (CDM conceptual model) details entities and relationships. |
| 3. Catalog and metadata layer (discovery layer) | Central catalog of datasets and products used in NCAIF, including metadata such as owner, source, spatial unit, update cadence, classification, limitations, access path, and legal mandate. This layer surfaces **what exists** and **how to obtain it**. | Subject‑area **data stewards** (for climate, risk, loss and damage, adaptation, resilience) coordinated by DCCE central data team. | Reviewed when datasets change or are newly onboarded; v1 emphasises that frequency differs by asset, with minimum expectations defined by governance gate G2. | Metadata for open datasets is public; catalog entries for G2G and internal datasets are visible at least inside DCCE and partner agencies. | Main report: Ch.1 §1.10 (emphasis on metadata, access levels, and transparency); Appendix ข §3.6 ("บัญชีข้อมูล การดาวน์โหลด และบริการเชื่อมต่อข้อมูล"). |
| 4. Recommended baseline registry (MVP‑3) | Registry that answers "Which dataset should I use for this question and at what scale?" Each entry records an endorsed dataset (or combination), intended use, spatial/temporal scope, limitations, version history, and access rail. | Jointly owned by **DCCE central data team** and **source‑agency focal points**; endorsement authority configured as an interim panel in Phase 1. | Updated whenever new baselines are endorsed, superseded, or when methods materially change; cadence linked to major updates in underlying datasets. | Mirrors underlying dataset’s rail (Open / G2G / Internal), but registry entries themselves are at least visible inside DCCE as part of governance documentation. | Main report: Ch.1 §1.10 (proposal for "ทะเบียนชุดข้อมูลอ้างอิงที่แนะนำให้ใช้" and status flags); Chapter‑1 review §1.11; governance strategy v3 (MVP‑3 baseline registry). |
| 5. NCAIF content and profile layer (presentation layer) | Structured NCAIF components such as Policy Maker Centre pages, adaptation‑cycle views, risk and area profiles, loss‑and‑damage views, and adaptation planning/implementation sections. Each component links back to catalog entries and recommended baselines and includes limitations and caveats. | **Section content owners** (e.g. policy team for Policy Maker Centre; technical teams for Risk & Area Profiles and loss‑and‑damage; adaptation team for planning and implementation), coordinated by NCAIF product owner. | Updated when important baselines change, after major events, and in line with agreed reporting cycles (e.g. annual updates for profiles). | Public where underlying data and narratives are suitable for open publication; selected content can be internal‑only where data are sensitive. | Main report: Ch.1 §1.9 (draft NCAIF structure, sitemap logic); Appendix ข §2–3 (menu structure and main page types: profiles, method pages, data‑service pages). |
| 6. User‑facing products and packs (export layer) | Exportable artifacts generated from NCAIF content and the baseline registry: briefing packs, budget‑justification packs, post‑event reports, sector briefs, and other decision‑support materials. | **Product owners** within DCCE (e.g. communications, technical analysis, disaster‑reporting units), following common templates and QA/QC rules. | Produced on demand or according to policy/reporting timetables; must be updated when underlying baselines or key narratives are revised. | Distributed in line with the most restrictive rail of the underlying data (public packs vs internal briefings). | Main report: executive summary (discussion of "ผลิตภัณฑ์ขั้นต่ำ" for decision support); Ch.1 §1.7, §1.9 (need for decision‑oriented products and exportable summaries); Appendix ค (use cases for briefing, budgeting, and post‑event analysis). |

This table stays within the tiers and roles implied by the v1 main report and appendices. It can be cited directly in Section 1 §5.2.5 as the **draft data‑management structure** without introducing new tiers beyond those supported by the CDM (Appendix ก) and NCAIF structure (Appendix ข).
