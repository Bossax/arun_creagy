# Workshop Design: Session 2 – The System Engineering Round

**Project**: CRDB (TOR 5.3.3 Consultation Workshop)  
**Target**: 80+ Stakeholders (20+ Organizations)  
**Duration**: 1 Hour 30 Minutes (Post-Coffee Break)  
**Atmosphere**: Collaborative Engineering / Problem Solving  

---

## 1. Goal and Narrative
The goal of this session is to move from "Inventory" to **"Activation."** Participants act as System Architects to repair a **"Broken Data-to-Action Chain."** Instead of abstract discussion, they use physical **Tool Cards** and **Building Blocks** to construct a realistic workflow that solves a specific hazard-related problem defined in Session 1.

## 2. Strategic Extraction (DCCE Value)
*   **The Utility Gap (TOR 5.3.8)**: Identifying which data/services are missing (Gold "Missing Piece" Cards).
*   **Governance Friction (TOR 5.3.3 & 5.3.9)**: Identifying institutional, legal, or technical barriers to information flow (Red "Friction Stickers").
*   **MVP Prioritization**: Identifying which products have the highest "Decision Utility" for non-technical users.

---

## 3. The Construction Materials

### A. The "Skeleton Canvas" (A1 Paper)
A linear workflow map with two fixed anchors:
*   **The Origin**: The Hazard (e.g., Flash Flood, Severe Heat).
*   **The Destination**: The Local Action (e.g., District Response, Budget Authorization).
*   **The Path**: 5–7 empty "Handshake Slots" for participants to fill.

### B. The "Tool Cards" (Data Supply)
Pre-printed cards based on the verified National Inventory. Each card contains **Reality Metadata**:
*   **Owner/Source**: (e.g., TMD, HII, MSDHS)
*   **Format**: (e.g., Static PDF, CSV, Live API, Web-View)
*   **Update Frequency**: (e.g., Daily, Real-time, Yearly)
*   **Known Constraint**: (e.g., "MOU Required," "High Latency," "Sensitive Data")

### C. The "Building Blocks" (Processes/Roles)
Cards representing the actions taken to transform data into a service:
*   **Knowledge Broker**: Translates technical science into policy briefs.
*   **Data Quality Auditor**: Validates data against national standards (DGA/NSO).
*   **Legal Handshake**: Handles MOUs and data-sharing agreements.
*   **Technical Modeler**: Downscales or aggregates raw data for local use.

---

## 4. The Building Rules (The "Reality Check")

Participants must navigate the "Managerial Zoom" (Institutional Handshake level). They cannot assume tools are perfect. For every connection made on the canvas, the **Non-Technical User** (Decision Owner) must perform a "Reality Audit" using three questions:

1.  **Accessibility**: "Can I actually see/get this data today?" (If no → Red Sticker: **Access**)
2.  **Usability**: "Is it in a format I can use without a PhD?" (If no → Red Sticker: **Usability**)
3.  **Delay**: "Does it arrive fast enough to change my decision?" (If no → Red Sticker: **Delay**)

*If the connection is missing a role or a data source, the team must write a **Gold Card** for the "Missing Piece."*

---

## 5. Session Timeline (90 Mins)

*   **00:00 - 00:15 | Briefing & Goal Setting**: Finalizing the **Problem Statement** from Session 1.
*   **00:15 - 01:00 | The Construction Phase**: Teams use Blocks and Tool Cards to "bridge the gap" from Hazard to Action. Facilitators guide negotiations.
*   **01:00 - 01:20 | The Stress Test**: Facilitator introduces a "2030 Crisis Twist" (e.g., "The main fiber-optic line is cut" or "The mayor needs an answer in 30 minutes"). Teams must explain how their system survives.
*   **01:20 - 01:30 | The Extraction**: Facilitators collect all **Red Friction Stickers** and **Gold Need Cards** for the DCCE Data Strategy report.
