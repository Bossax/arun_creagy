## 0) Output you want to walk away with (so questions stay disciplined)

For each MVP (1–4), capture:

1) **Which service level is most useful now** (L1 pragmatic vs L2 ideal)
2) **Which decision/job it supports** (budgeting, planning, zoning, design standard, procurement, reporting)
3) **What is missing** (data inputs, impact variables, sector modules, thresholds, metadata)
4) **What blocks adoption** (access rules, mandates, capacity, trust/endorsement, timeliness)
5) **What is the minimum viable next step** (what can DCCE do first without over-promising)

These align to the Activity 2 matrix in [`2026-04-30_crdb-workshop-master-design-v2.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/consultation_workshop/2026-04-30_crdb-workshop-master-design-v2.md:178).

## 1) Mentimeter question set (pre-discussion pulse)

### Q1 — Role lens (segmentation; 1 question)
**Question**: “Which role best describes you today?”

**Options** (keep short):
1) Data provider / steward
2) Analyst / researcher
3) Planner / policy (provincial / sector)
4) Operations / disaster response
5) Budget / reporting / M&E

**Why**: lets you interpret later answers by persona mix (avoid misreading “L2 is useless” when the room is non-technical).

---

### Q2 — Overall priority across the 4 MVPs (ranking; 1 question)
**Question**: “Which MVP would create the biggest immediate improvement for your work in the next 12 months?”

**Options**:
- MVP-1 Policy Briefing Service
- MVP-2 Disaster Data Pipeline / Climate disaster statistics
- MVP-3 Endorsed Data Inventory (catalog)
- MVP-4 Uncertainty / Decision-readiness Shield

**Format**: ranking (1–4) or single-choice if time is tight.

---

### Q3–Q6 — One question per MVP (avoid “depends”)
Run **exactly one** Mentimeter question after each MVP explanation.

Goal: avoid the predictable “depends” answer by forcing participants to pick a **single concrete trigger/need/boundary**.

#### Q3 (MVP-1)
**Question**: “For **MVP-1 (Briefing Service)**, what must be on the **front page** for it to be usable?”

**Options** (pick **up to 2**):
1) 1 headline message (what to worry about + why it matters)
2) 1 map (hotspot / priority area)
3) 3 key numbers (e.g., trend, affected population/assets, cost/risk rank)
4) ‘How to use safely’ box (limitations + do-not-use-for)
5) Data source + last updated (version)

#### Q4 (MVP-2)
**Question**: “For **MVP-2 (Disaster data)**, what is the *minimum* output that would already be useful?”

**Options**:
1) A clean **table** for reporting (standard categories)
2) A **dashboard** showing hotspots + trends
3) A **map layer** for planning tools
4) A **method note** defining categories/terms (so reporting is consistent)

#### Q5 (MVP-3)
**Question**: “For **MVP-3 (Endorsed inventory/catalog)**, what is the single most important thing you need from the catalog?”

**Options**:
1) “Which dataset is **official to use** for reporting/planning?”
2) “Who is the **owner/contact** and how do I request access?”
3) “What are the **limitations / safe-use rules**?”
4) “Which dataset fits **my specific job** (sector/use case)?”

#### Q6 (MVP-4)
**Question**: “For **MVP-4 (Uncertainty/Decision shield)**, which decision boundary do you most need the system to protect?”

**Options**:
1) Prevent misuse in **engineering/design** decisions
2) Prevent misuse in **public communication/media**
3) Prevent misuse in **budgeting / policy justification**
4) Prevent misuse in **local planning / zoning**

**Note**: These questions still preserve the workshop intent (Decision Utility + barriers) but generate **actionable distributions** instead of “we need both”.

---

### Q7 — Biggest adoption blocker (single-choice; 1 question)
**Question**: “What is the biggest blocker to using any of these services in real work?”

**Options**:
1) Access / legal restrictions / MOUs
2) Data quality / inconsistent definitions
3) Lack of endorsement / trust (“which number is official?”)
4) Timeliness / update frequency
5) Capacity (people/time/skills)
6) Not aligned with our workflow / decision cycle

---

### Q8 — Minimum trust signal (single-choice; 1 question)
**Question**: “What must be visible for you to reuse an output from NCAIF/CRDB in a briefing/report?”

**Options** (pick 4–5 max):
1) Source agency + contact
2) Last updated / version
3) Limitations + ‘what not to do’ guidance
4) Endorsement / sign-off status (and by whom)
5) Method summary (plain language)

**Why**: forces concrete “trust UI” requirements (connects to MVP-3 and MVP-4 in [`mvp_grounded_dummies_v2_narrative_cards.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/consultation_workshop/mvp/mvp_grounded_dummies_v2_narrative_cards.md:87)).

---

### Q9 — Open text “missing impact variable” (word cloud; 1 question)
**Question**: “What **impact variable / indicator** is most critical for your sector that is missing today?”

**Prompt examples** (read aloud, not options): crops, heat-health outcomes, transport disruption, critical facilities, household vulnerability.

## 2) Breakout guiding questions (table discussion) — disciplined, worksheet-aligned

### Setup (script line)
“Use the worksheet. For each MVP, pick the **decision** first. Then choose the **service level** that makes that decision possible. Then tell us what is missing and what blocks it.”

This follows the Decision Utility Matrix in [`2026-04-30_crdb-workshop-master-design-v2.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/consultation_workshop/2026-04-30_crdb-workshop-master-design-v2.md:178) and the MVP table prompts in [`mvp_grounded_dummies_v2_narrative_cards.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/consultation_workshop/mvp/mvp_grounded_dummies_v2_narrative_cards.md:47).

---

### A) Cross-MVP “Decision Utility Matrix” prompts (use for each MVP)
1) **Decision / job-to-do**: “Name one real decision you make where this MVP would help (budget request, plan, zoning, standard, procurement, reporting).”
2) **Pick the level**: “For that decision, do you need Level 1 or Level 2? Why?”
3) **Minimum output**: “What exactly must be on the output for it to be usable (3 maps/3 numbers/1 label)?”
4) **Missing logic**: “What is missing for your sector (impact variables, thresholds, exposure layers, admin breakdown, narrative modules)?”
5) **Adoption blocker**: “What will stop you from using it next month (access, endorsement, capacity, timeliness, skills)?”
6) **Fastest viable first step**: “What could DCCE deliver first in 3–6 months without over-promising?”

---

### B) MVP-specific guiding questions (one sharp question each)

#### MVP-1 (Policy Briefing Service)
**Guiding question**: “What are the **3 front-page elements** your boss needs to sign off (map/number/message), and at what admin level (province/district/municipality)?”

*Evidence anchor*: Level 1 vs Level 2 purpose in [`mvp_grounded_dummies_v2_narrative_cards.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/consultation_workshop/mvp/mvp_grounded_dummies_v2_narrative_cards.md:23) and deeper stakeholder justification in [`mvp1_stakeholder_fit_traceability_analysis.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/consultation_workshop/mvp/mvp1_stakeholder_fit_traceability_analysis.md:12).

#### MVP-2 (Disaster Data Pipeline / Climate disaster statistics)
**Guiding question**: “When you receive disaster/impact data, what are the **top 2 red flags** you must fix manually (definitions, location coding, duplicates, timing, damage categories)?”

*Evidence anchor*: MVP-2 prompts in [`mvp_grounded_dummies_v2_narrative_cards.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/consultation_workshop/mvp/mvp_grounded_dummies_v2_narrative_cards.md:55).

#### MVP-3 (Endorsed Data Inventory / Catalog)
**Guiding question**: “In your workflow, what does **‘endorsed’** mean *operationally* (who signs off, how often reviewed, what happens when datasets conflict)?”

*Evidence anchor*: MVP-3 prompts in [`mvp_grounded_dummies_v2_narrative_cards.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/consultation_workshop/mvp/mvp_grounded_dummies_v2_narrative_cards.md:87).

#### MVP-4 (Uncertainty / Decision-readiness Shield)
**Guiding question**: “What **decision labels** do you need (scoping/budgeting/zoning/design), and what must happen when a label is **Red** (escalation / consultation / prohibition)?”

*Evidence anchor*: MVP-4 prompts in [`mvp_grounded_dummies_v2_narrative_cards.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/consultation_workshop/mvp/mvp_grounded_dummies_v2_narrative_cards.md:119).
