## 1. THE STRATEGIC PIVOT
The design of the CRDB Consultation Workshop has undergone a fundamental shift from the previous "4 Product Concept Suites" model (Strategic Briefing, Real-Time, Community Resilience, and Financial Risk). [previous version](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/consultation_workshop/2026-04-27_CRDB-Morning-Workshop-Complete-Design)

### Rationale for the Pivot
The previous plan was identified as "sloppish" because it premised national coordination on pre-baked, abstract concepts that prioritized aesthetic "website features" over fundamental data utility. By presenting stakeholders with predefined "Suites," we risked **Expert Drift**—a state where technical consultants design for an idealized user who does not exist in the current administrative reality.

### The New Direction: A Reality Audit (Beyond Compliance)
The new design abandons these pre-baked templates in favor of a **Reality Audit** methodology. This shift ensures the workshop avoids boredom and technical dominance while pushing beyond the formal requirements of **TOR 5.3.3, 5.3.4, 5.3.5, and 5.3.8** to achieve true **National Capability Building**:

*   **From Lists to Logic**: Instead of just listing datasets (5.3.4), we map the **Operational Triggers** that turn raw data into a decision.
*   **From Discussion to Evidence**: Instead of "brainstorming" (5.3.3), the physical **Friction Flags** provide empirical evidence for the **Data Governance Strategy (5.3.9)**.
*   **From Meeting to Network**: The "Marketplace" and "System Repair" mechanics build **Shared Ownership**, creating a long-term "Resilience Network."

This pivot anchors the workshop in **Strategic Peer Inventory** and **System Engineering**, using reality-based metadata to bridge the gap between scientific supply and operational demand.

## 2. PITFALLS AVOIDED
This design is forensic by nature, specifically structured to bypass the three core failure modes of national data projects:

### A. Avoiding "Expert Drift"
We treat consultant "best guesses" as hallucinations until verified by current file evidence. Instead of asking agencies what they *might* want in a future system, we inventory what they are *currently* using to make decisions. This anchors the CRDB in empirical baseline data rather than speculative requirements.

### B. Neutralizing "Administrative Silos" (NAP Sectors)
Data does not naturally reside in vertical "NAP sector" chimneys (e.g., Agriculture vs. Health). Forcing participants into these traditional silos creates redundant datasets and prevents cross-sectoral resilience. This design uses "Mission-Led" grouping, where data flows across sectors to solve specific hazard-related problems (e.g., how water data informs health heat-maps).

### C. Preventing "Fantasy Engineering"
We avoid the trap of designing systems that look perfect in a technical proposal but fail in the field. By using the "System Engineering" round to "Repair the Chain," we force participants to identify the specific friction points—jargon, access rules, or file formats—that currently break the information flow.

### D. Eliminating the "Survey-Filling Trap"
Traditional workshops often devolve into passive "information gathering" sessions where stakeholders are treated as survey respondents. This leads to consultation fatigue and disconnected, low-fidelity results. This design uses **Active Construction** (building a physical map) instead of **Passive Reporting**. Stakeholders generate data by *experiencing* the friction of building a system in real-time. The gaps and technical requirements emerge naturally from the work, ensuring the output is grounded in operational truth.

## 4. ACTIVITY SUMMARIES

### Session 1: Strategic Peer Inventory
*   **Objective**: To conduct an empirical audit of current climate risk data assets across 39+ organizations.
*   **Format**: Table-based mapping using "Asset Posters" and "Demand Stickers."
*   **What People Do**: 
    *   **Operational Reveals**: Instead of a generic introduction, each person has 2 minutes to state one data "Asset" they provide and one "Operational Hurdle" they face daily. 
    *   **Mirror Audit**: Participants physically walk to the "Mirror Posters" on the wall to verify their agency's data listings, using **Correction Stickers** to add missing sources or flag "Ghost Data" that is listed but inaccessible.
    *   **Problem Anchoring**: Facilitators help the group agree on a single, shared "Problem Statement" for their cluster to solve in the next round.
*   **Outputs**: A verified national inventory of baseline datasets and operational "User Triggers."
*   **Detailed Design**: See [[ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/consultation_workshop/2026-04-28_CRDB-Session1-Strategic-Peer-Inventory-Design|2026-04-28_CRDB-Session1-Strategic-Peer-Inventory-Design]]

### Session 2: System Engineering (Repairing the Chain)
*   **Objective**: To move from static inventory to active workflow construction, identifying and fixing friction points.
*   **Format**: "Architecture Building Blocks" activity using physical tool cards to map a specific information flow.
*   **What People Do**: 
    *   **Chain Assembly**: Teams use physical **Tool Cards** (Data sources with reality-metadata) and **Building Blocks** (Processes/Roles like "Legal Handshake") to fill the gaps in a "Skeleton Canvas."
    *   **Reality Audit**: The designated "Decision Owner" (Mayors/Directors) at the table must "approve" each connection by asking if it is actually accessible, usable, and timely.
    *   **Friction Tagging**: If a connection fails the audit, the team must place a **Red Friction Sticker** on the canvas and describe the hurdle (e.g., "Missing MOU").
    *   **Crisis Twist**: In the final 20 minutes, a "2030 Crisis Scenario" is introduced, forcing the team to explain how their constructed system survives a sudden data outage or rapid decision deadline.
*   **Outputs**: Documented "Data-to-Action Chains" with specific policy and technical "Unlocks" identified.
*   **Detailed Design**: See [[ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/consultation_workshop/2026-04-28_CRDB-Session2-System-Engineering-Design|2026-04-28_CRDB-Session2-System-Engineering-Design]]
