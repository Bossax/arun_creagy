# Workshop Guide: MVP Presentation & Feedback Structure
**Project**: Climate Resilience Data Board (CRDB / NCAIF)
**Goal**: Validate Phase 1 requirements, confirm institutional fit, and discover missing workflow links.

---

## 1. Global Narrative: "From Data Piles to Decision Services"
Before presenting individual MVPs, set the stage:
*   **The Problem**: We have plenty of data, but it’s "trapped" in PDFs, broken websites, or conflicting spreadsheets.
*   **The Vision**: CRDB isn't just a website; it’s a **Decision Support Framework** that ensures data is trusted, timely, and tailored to *your* specific job.

---

## 2. Standard MVP Presentation Module (The "Four-Box" Framework)
Each of the 4 MVPs should be presented using this identical structure to maintain participant focus.

### Box 1: The "Pain Point" (Why are we doing this?)
*   *Narrative*: "We heard from [Agency X] that [Specific Problem] happens every time an event occurs."
*   *Visual*: A simple "Current Reality" diagram (e.g., "Manual Excel requests flying back and forth").

### Box 2: The "Concept" (What is it?)
*   *Definition*: A 1-sentence plain-language summary.
*   *Key Feature*: The single most important "Value-Add" (e.g., "The official seal of approval").

### Box 3: The "User Journey" (How does it feel to use it?)
*   *Walkthrough*: A 3-step storyboard.
    1.  **Trigger**: "I need to prepare a budget brief for the Governor."
    2.  **Action**: "I log in, select my province, and click 'Generate Briefing Pack'."
    3.  **Output**: "I get a 2-page PDF with maps, data, and mandatory caveats already included."

### Box 4: The "Governance Rule" (Who is responsible?)
*   *Clarification*: "DCCE doesn't own this data. [Agency Y] provides it; DCCE ensures it passes the **Quality Gate**."

---

## 3. Specific MVP Briefing Sheets

### MVP-1: Policy Briefing Service (The "Exporter")
*   **Target Persona**: Executives, Provincial Planners, Local Authorities.
*   **Discovery Question**: "What are the 3 numbers or maps that *must* be on the front page for your boss to sign off?"

### MVP-2: Disaster Data Pipeline (The "Intake")
*   **Target Persona**: DDPM Operators, Data Stewards, Field Officers.
*   **Discovery Question**: "When you receive raw data from the field, what are the most common 'red flags' you have to manually fix?"

### MVP-3: Endorsed Data Inventory (The "Catalog")
*   **Target Persona**: Analysts, Researchers, Consultants.
*   **Discovery Question**: "When two agencies give you different numbers for 'Flood Risk,' how do you currently decide which one to trust?"

### MVP-4: Uncertainty Data Shield (The "Interpreter")
*   **Target Persona**: PR Teams, Media, General Public.
*   **Discovery Question**: "What is the biggest fear your agency has when publishing 'predicted' data to the public?"

---

## 5. Probing for "New Discovery"
Use these "What-If" prompts to close each session:
*   "If this system was perfect on Day 1, what is the *first* thing you would do with it that you can't do today?"
*   "Who is the one person in your agency who would *refuse* to use this, and why?" (This uncovers hidden governance/cultural barriers).
