## 1. Strategic Framing: From Design Review to Feasibility Audit
The afternoon session is the formal **Public Hearing (TOR 5.2.6)**. It is not designed to secure binding organizational commitments, as participants may not have the authority to make such decisions on the spot. Instead, its purpose is a **Technical Feasibility Assessment** of the draft framework and management structure.

Our goal is to extract a **Gap Analysis for Implementation** by enabling participants to act as **Subject Matter Experts**, identifying specific, real-world challenges.

## 4. Activity Detail: Extracting the Gap Analysis

### Session 3: Framework Categorization Review ("Unclassified Data" Identification)
*   **Objective**: To identify **Taxonomy Gaps** by reviewing the NCAIF sitemap.
*   **The "Information Provided"**: We present a large, physical poster of the NCAIF sitemap (vNext).
*   **The "Input Required" / Activity**: Participants take their key "Dataset" sticky notes from the morning and must find a logical categorization for them on the sitemap.
*   **Extraction**:
    *   Any dataset that cannot be clearly categorized highlights **Data Categorization Gaps.** The table must document *why* it does not fit.
    *   Any section of the map with no associated datasets indicates areas of low relevance or coverage.
    *   Facilitators will document these categorization discrepancies as evidence.

### Session 4: Governance Implementation Assessment ("Assessment of Implementation Challenges")
*   **Objective**: To identify **Implementation Barriers** and **High-Value Features**.
*   **The "Information Provided"**: We present the 3 "Phase 1 Minimum Rules" (Metadata, Baseline Endorsement, Limitations Shield) as distinct "Service Requirements."
*   **The "Input Required" / Activity**: For each rule, tables use two types of stickers:
    *   **Red "Implementation Barrier" Indicator**: Placed on a rule they believe is **costly or impractical** to comply with. They must document the specific blocker (e.g., "Lack of dedicated staff," "Legal approval process," "Existing systems do not support this field").
    *   **Green "Identified Benefit" Indicator**: Placed on a service that provides **significant, immediate value** to their agency (e.g., the "Limitations Shield" feature helps protect their agency from misinterpretation of data).
*   **Extraction**:
    *   The concentration of **Red Indicators** provides a prioritized list of real-world implementation risks.
    *   The concentration of **Green Indicators** highlights services with strong user demand and perceived benefit.

## 5. Expected Outputs for CRDB (The "Mandate")
By the end of the afternoon, we will have extracted:
1.  **A Validated Taxonomy Gap Report**: A list of specific data types the NCAIF sitemap fails to categorize or adequately represent.

3.  **A Utility-Driven Feature Roadmap**: The services that generated the most "Identified Benefit" indicators, providing the CRDB team with a clear, user-validated development priority list.
