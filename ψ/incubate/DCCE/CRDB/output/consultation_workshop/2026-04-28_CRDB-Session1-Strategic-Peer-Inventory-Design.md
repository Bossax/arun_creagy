# Workshop Design: Session 1 – Strategic Peer Inventory

**Project**: CRDB (TOR 5.3.3 Consultation Workshop)  
**Target**: 80+ Stakeholders (20+ Organizations)  
**Duration**: 30–40 Minutes (Pre-Coffee Break)  
**Atmosphere**: Professional Line Agency Consultation  

---

## 1. Goal and Narrative
The goal of this session is to move from a room of individual agencies to a series of **"Operational Hazard Clusters."** It establishes a shared "Basemap" for the table based on current professional reality. We avoid abstract "icebreakers" in favor of an **"Operational Reveal"** that respects the seniority and technical expertise in the room.

## 2. Strategic Extraction (DCCE Value)
*   **Inventory Verification (TOR 5.3.4/5)**: Capturing real-time corrections to the pre-prepared National Data & Service Map.
*   **Initial Gap Analysis (TOR 5.3.8)**: Identifying the primary "Operational Friction" points that drive the need for the CRDB.

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Workflow

### Step 1: The "I Have / I Need" Professional Reveal (20-25 Mins)
Each participant is given 2 minutes to introduce themselves through the lens of their agency's role in the specific Hazard Cluster (e.g., Water Security, Thermal Health).

*   **The "Current Asset"**: "My agency currently provides [X] specific data or service relevant to this cluster."
*   **The "Current Operation"**: "In my daily work, the biggest hurdle to using climate information for this sector is [Y]."
*   **Capture Tool**: Facilitators record these on the **Table Alignment Map** (A1 Canvas).

### Step 2: Cluster Alignment & Problem Definition (10 Mins)
The Facilitator synthesizes the "I Have/I Need" reveals to find the common denominator at the table.

*   **The Prompt**: "It appears that the majority of our agencies are focused on [Z]. Can we agree that building a shared workflow for [Z] will provide the most value for all represented roles today?"
*   **Output**: Completion of the **Problem Statement Template**:
    *   **The Mission**: We need to solve [X] for [Y] community.
    *   **The Decision Owner**: (Identifying the primary user role at the table).
    *   **The Target Condition**: Success looks like...

### Step 3: The Baseline Audit (Ongoing/Concurrent)
The table is presented with the **"Mirror Posters"**—the existing CRDB inventory of data and platforms for their specific cluster.

*   **The Validation**: Participants use **Correction Stickers** to update the map:
    *   *Checkmark*: "I verify this source is the current National Standard."
    *   *Question Mark*: "This data is listed but is not actionable in my daily work."
    *   *New Card*: "My agency has [X] which is missing from this map."

---

## 4. Facilitator Guidance
*   **Maintain Hierarchy**: Ensure high-level representatives from line agencies feel their "Asset" (authority/data) is respected and correctly mapped.
*   **Bridge the Gap**: Actively connect the "Data Providers" (Scientists) with the "Decision Owners" (Mayors/Directors) during the problem definition phase.
*   **Avoid "Feature" Talk**: If participants start talking about "Website buttons," pull them back to the **Information Flow**: *"Who gives what to whom so that action happens?"*
