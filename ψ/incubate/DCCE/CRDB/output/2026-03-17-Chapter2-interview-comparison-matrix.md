# Chapter 2 interview comparison matrix

Purpose: create one normalized view of what different agencies already produce or manage, how they work with data, where constraints appear, and what Chapter 2 should extract from those interviews.

## Normalization notes

- `Update frequency` uses broad categories because the interview evidence is uneven.
- `Scale` records the most decision-relevant scale mentioned by each agency, not every possible scale in their systems.
- `Needs / use cases` condense only the points most relevant to Chapter 2 drafting.

## Cross-cutting observations from the matrix

1. **Agencies are not all “data producers” in the same way.** Some generate primary data (NSO, DDPM), some coordinate or govern data (DGA, NXPO), and some are heavy users who require decision-ready transformation (NESDC, OTP, TBA).
2. **Spatial needs diverge sharply.** Policy users can sometimes work with provincial summaries, but operational and financial users repeatedly ask for municipality, sub-district, corridor, or asset-level detail.
3. **Metadata and discovery are a recurring pain point.** MSDHS, NSO, DGA, and FGD1 feedback all reinforce the need for better dictionaries, cataloging, and owner pathways.
4. **Forward-looking projections are a recurring unmet need.** DLA, MSDHS, NESDC, OTP, and TBA all need future-oriented risk information for planning, budgeting, or stress testing.
5. **Economic loss remains a major weak point.** DDPM, NESDC, and TBA each reveal a different side of the same gap: current systems do not adequately translate hazard and impact data into defensible economic-loss estimates.
