# Chapter 2 use-case clustering

Purpose: cluster the scattered uses of climate risk and impact information into a compact set of practical groups that Chapter 2 can describe without retelling each interview separately.

## Use-case clusters

| Use-case cluster | Core question agencies are trying to answer | Representative agencies | Main data types required | Why this matters for Chapter 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Emergency response and post-event assessment** | Where did the event happen, who and what were affected, and what action or relief should follow? | DDPM, MSDHS, DCCE internal workflows | event records, hazard context, affected population, affected assets, relief and impact records | shows immediate operational use of climate-risk and disaster data | 
| **Local planning and budget justification** | What evidence can local governments use to justify resilience spending, local plans, and implementation choices? | DLA, LAOs, DCCE | local risk layers, exposed assets and people, baseline indicators, exportable summaries | links information use directly to planning and annual budget decisions |
| **Social protection and vulnerable-group targeting** | Which vulnerable populations are exposed, where are they located, and what support or relocation planning is needed? | MSDHS, local welfare actors | vulnerable-group registries, sub-district overlays, subgroup-sensitive social indicators, hazard layers | shows that climate information is used for targeted welfare and service delivery, not only engineering |
| **Macroeconomic planning and national evaluation** | How do climate risks affect growth, sector performance, and national policy evaluation? | NESDC, NXPO, NSO | macroeconomic baselines, sector accounts, exposure baselines, verified indicators, loss-and-damage estimates | demonstrates use of climate information for national planning and policy appraisal |
| **Infrastructure resilience planning** | Which transport or infrastructure assets are exposed, how severe is the disruption risk, and what investment response is justified? | OTP, transport-sector planners | high-resolution hazard projections, corridor and asset data, critical-route exposure, engineering baselines | shows that some sectors need much finer resolution than province-level summaries |
| **Financial-sector risk analysis** | How do physical hazards affect portfolios, collateral, and supply-chain stability under stress scenarios? | Thai Bankers' Association / commercial banks | asset-level hazard data, probabilistic flood maps, damage functions, sector mappings, uncertainty guidance | demonstrates that climate-risk information is already being translated into financial decision contexts |

## Expanded interpretation of each cluster

### 1. Emergency response and post-event assessment

This cluster is centered on **operational immediacy**. DDPM and related actors need data for event declaration, impact reporting, relief coordination, and post-event review. MSDHS intersects here when vulnerable-group protection requires detailed local action during crises.

### 2. Local planning and budget justification

This cluster is strongly visible in DLA and related local-government concerns. The core need is not merely access to maps, but access to **defensible evidence packs** that can support annual budget requests, project justification, and alignment with official planning systems.

### 3. Social protection and vulnerable-group targeting

This cluster highlights that climate-risk information is not only about hazards and infrastructure. MSDHS needs to connect climate risk to vulnerable populations, service pathways, shelter planning, and potentially relocation support.

### 4. Macroeconomic planning and national evaluation

NESDC, NXPO, and NSO show that climate information is also used to answer high-level questions about national development, GDP, SDG progress, infrastructure appraisal, and whether the country is actually becoming more resilient.

### 5. Infrastructure resilience planning

OTP reveals a use case where standard national layers are often too coarse. In this cluster, users need detailed hazard interpretation aligned to specific transport corridors and critical nodes, not only broad administrative summaries.

### 6. Financial-sector risk analysis

Banks use climate-risk information to evaluate exposure and stress test portfolios. This is a distinct use-case cluster because it needs asset-level precision, probabilistic interpretation, and stronger damage functions than most policy uses currently require.
