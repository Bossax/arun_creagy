# 5.5.2 Coverage Gaps Note – List A and List B

## 1. Framing

Task 5.5 expects the project to demonstrate:

- A **10-year review** of climate-risk evidence across the **six NAP sectors**; and
- A **shortlist of ≥10 significant studies** (List B) that can underpin NCAIF minimum content and CRDB products.

Using the completed List A inventory and the 5.5.1 Evidence Review Matrix, List B has been constructed as a curated subset focusing on decision-relevant, methodologically robust, and policy-linked studies. This note compares the **intended coverage** from the Task 5.5 scope with the **actual coverage** of List A and List B, and identifies remaining gaps.

## 2. Coverage Overview

### 2.1 Sectoral coverage (NAP 6)

- **Agriculture and Food Security:** Well represented through L1, L2, L4, L5, L6, L7, L11, which together cover crop impacts, water interactions, and haze-related agricultural risks.
- **Water Resources Management:** Strong coverage via L1, L2, L3, L4, L6, L7, providing both national syntheses and basin-scale case studies.
- **Human Settlements and Security / Urban Infrastructure and Industry:** Addressed through BMR-focused flood and exposure studies (L4, L8, L9) and heat/air-quality work (L10).
- **Public Health:** Covered by heat and air-quality studies (L5, L10) and haze-related evidence (L11, L12).
- **Natural Resource Management:** Addressed indirectly via biomass-burning and haze evidence (L11, L12) but less so for ecosystems, forests, and coastal resources.
- **Tourism:** Represented indirectly via flood and haze impacts (L4, L9, L11, L12), but dedicated tourism-sector climate-risk studies are limited.

### 2.2 Hazard coverage

- **Drought and water scarcity:** Present via basin and crop-level modelling (L1, L2, L3, L4, L6, L7) but concentrated in agricultural and water-supply contexts.
- **Flooding and extreme rainfall:** Well covered for national indices and BMR case studies (L3, L4, L8, L9), including depth–damage evidence.
- **Heat and humid heat:** Captured via national trends and BMR-focused work (L4, L5, L10) but with limited direct health-outcome quantification.
- **Air pollution / haze (PM2.5):** Covered through national and northern-region studies (L10, L11, L12).
- **Coastal hazards and sea-level rise:** Only lightly represented (L3 includes some coastal content); dedicated coastal-inundation or erosion studies are under-represented.

### 2.3 Geographies and scales

- **Bangkok Metropolitan Region and Central plains:** Strongly represented (L2, L4, L5, L8, L9, L10, L11).
- **Northern Thailand:** Moderately represented, especially for water and haze (L1, L3, L4, L5, L6, L11, L12), but with gaps on highland socio-economic impacts.
- **Northeastern Thailand (Chi–Mun system):** Represented via hydrologic modelling (L4, L5, L7, L11) but with limited sector-specific vulnerability analysis.
- **Southern and Eastern regions:** Included in some national-scale analyses (L3, L4, L5, L11) but under-represented in detailed case studies.
- **National-scale syntheses:** L3, L4, L5, L11 provide broad coverage but still leave some sector–region combinations with sparse evidence.

### 2.4 Time horizons and scenarios

- **Historical baselines and recent decades:** Well covered, providing observational context and recent-event analogues (e.g., 2011 floods, recent haze seasons).
- **Mid-century and late-century projections:** Covered for key basins and crops (L1, L2, L4, L6, L7) but not uniformly across all sectors and regions.
- **Scenario diversity:** Multiple SSPs/RCPs are used across studies, but there is no single consistent scenario set spanning all sectors.

## 3. Identified Gaps

Based on the Task 5.5 expectations and the current List A/B coverage, the following gaps are notable:

1. **Coastal and marine systems:**
   - Limited dedicated evidence on sea-level rise, coastal erosion, saline intrusion, and marine ecosystem impacts along Thailand’s coasts.
   - Implication: NCAIF coastal content and CRDB coastal indicators will need either additional evidence-gathering or careful framing of current limitations.

2. **Ecosystems and Natural Resource Management beyond haze:**
   - Few studies focused explicitly on forest ecosystems, biodiversity, and ecosystem-based adaptation under climate change.
   - Implication: NAP natural-resource sector narratives may rely more on global/regional evidence or complementary national assessments not yet in List A.

3. **Tourism-specific impact studies:**
   - Tourism appears mainly as an indirect impact channel (flood disruptions, haze seasons), with limited dedicated tourism-risk studies.
   - Implication: Tourism-sector pages in NCAIF may need to lean on qualitative interpretation and cross-sector evidence rather than sector-specific studies.

4. **Urban heat and health outcome quantification:**
   - While heat and air-quality hazards are documented (L4, L5, L10, L11, L12), there is limited direct quantification of health outcomes (e.g., morbidity/mortality) or adaptation effectiveness in Thai cities.
   - Implication: Health-sector adaptation narratives can highlight this gap and point to priority areas for future work.

5. **Regional balance for South and East:**
   - Detailed case studies for Southern and Eastern provinces are under-represented; they are mostly present within national analyses.
   - Implication: Region-specific risk narratives for these areas will require cautious extrapolation from national indices and similar-region analogues.

## 4. Implications for Future Evidence Work

Within the scope of this project, gaps are **documented but not closed**. Future evidence work could consider:

- Targeted **coastal risk studies** (sea-level rise, saline intrusion, coastal flooding) for key coastal provinces and economic zones.
- **Ecosystem-focused assessments** linking climate impacts to forests, biodiversity, and ecosystem services, including the role of nature-based solutions.
- **Tourism-sector climate-risk studies** focused on seasonality, visitor behaviour, and climate-related disruptions (floods, heat, haze) in major destinations.
- **Health outcome modelling** for heat and air quality in urban centres, combining epidemiological data with climate and pollution projections.
- Additional **case studies in Southern and Eastern regions** to complement existing basin and BMR-focused work.

These directions should be framed in the interim report and NCAIF content as **priorities for future evidence and system development**, not as commitments to specific new studies within the current project.
