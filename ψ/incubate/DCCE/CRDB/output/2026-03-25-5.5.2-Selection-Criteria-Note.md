# 5.5.2 Selection Criteria – List B Significant Studies

## 1. Purpose of List B Relative to List A

List A provides a **broad inventory** of authoritative climate-risk studies and related data architectures for Thailand during 2016–2026, mapped systematically across sectors, hazards, geographies, time horizons, and NCAIF content slots in the evidence-review matrix.

List B is a **curated subset** of List A that:

- Highlights the most **decision-relevant and illustrative studies** for CRDB and NCAIF Phase 1 products.
- Ensures **minimum sectoral and hazard coverage** across the 6 NAP sectors, while avoiding unnecessary duplication.
- Provides a concrete backbone for **Section 3 narrative inserts, NCAIF content mapping, and example use cases** for decision-makers.

List B is therefore not a second literature review; it is a **selection and framing step** over the completed 5.5.1 inventory and matrix.

## 2. Selection Criteria (Applied to List A)

The following criteria were applied jointly (not sequentially) to the List A inventory and its evidence-review matrix. A study is included in List B where it scores strongly on several of these dimensions, while also contributing to overall coverage balance.

### 2.1 Sectoral coverage and representativeness (NAP 6)

- Covers at least one of the NAP adaptation sectors: **Agriculture and Food Security, Water Resources Management, Human Settlements and Security, Public Health, Natural Resource Management, Tourism**.
- Helps ensure that, taken together, List B spans **all or nearly all sectors**, with particular attention to:
  - Water–agriculture interactions (L1, L2, L3, L6, L7).
  - Urban and industrial systems (L8, L9, L10).
  - Health and natural resources (L10, L11, L12).

### 2.2 Hazard and geography coverage

- Contributes to a **balanced hazard portfolio** across drought, flood/extreme rainfall, coastal/erosion, heat/thermal stress, and air-pollution/haze.
- Strengthens **geographical coverage** across Thailand:
  - **Northern and Northeastern basins** (L1, L3, L4, L5, L6, L7, L11, L12).
  - **Bangkok Metropolitan Region and Central plains** (L2, L4, L5, L8, L9, L10, L11).
  - **Multi-region or national scale syntheses** (L3, L4, L5, L11).
- Preference for studies that clearly specify **scale and units** that can be re-used in CRI Thailand and NCAIF visualizations.

### 2.3 Methodological robustness and authority

- Uses **peer-reviewed methods** (e.g., process-based modelling, SWAT–MODFLOW, WRF-Chem, systematic reviews, statistical trend and index analysis) with transparent assumptions.
- Published in **reputable international journals** or outputs led by recognised Thai research institutions.
- Provides **quantitative indicators** (e.g., % yield change, runoff shifts, depth–damage functions, PM2.5 contributions) that can be translated into NCAIF narratives and map explainers.

### 2.4 Relevance to NCAIF minimum content and CRDB products

- Aligns clearly with the **NCAIF minimum content slots** captured in the evidence-review matrix (climate foundations, sector risk & adaptation narratives, hazard explainers, decision-maker briefs).
- Offers **direct hooks** to CRDB’s role as a climate-risk database and to CRI Thailand development (e.g., hazard indices, exposure metrics, loss/damage evidence).
- Supports **Phase 1 deliverables** without requiring entirely new data pipelines (e.g., uses already-public datasets or evidence that can be summarised textually).

### 2.5 Usefulness for decision-maker-oriented examples

- Contains **clear storylines or case studies** that can be turned into short boxes, map explainers, or briefs (e.g., Lower Chao Phraya rice adaptation options, BMR flood exposure trends, Northern haze episodes).
- Helps illustrate **trade-offs or co-benefits** (e.g., surface–groundwater interactions, heat–air quality interactions, flood–drought co-occurrence).
- Enables **cross-sector narratives** that show how CRDB and NCAIF can support integrated decision-making (e.g., agriculture–water–health links).

## 3. Application Process

1. **Starting set:** Begin with all List A entries (`L1`–`L12`) as defined in the 5.5.1 Source Inventory table.
2. **Matrix-based screening:** For each study, review its row in the 5.5.1 Evidence Review Matrix, focusing on:
   - Sector labels and NCAIF page relevance.
   - Hazard and geographic coverage.
   - Strength and clarity of the "key findings" and "policy relevance" signals.
3. **Shortlist construction:** Mark studies that:
   - Score strongly on methodological robustness and policy relevance; and
   - Fill important sector–hazard–geography combinations not already well represented in the emerging shortlist.
4. **Balance check:** Adjust the shortlist to:
   - Avoid over-concentrating on a single basin or hazard when several similar studies exist.
   - Ensure that, collectively, List B spans **multiple NAP sectors, multiple hazards, and both regional case studies and national syntheses**.
5. **Traceability:** For each List B entry, maintain:
   - The **original `id` from List A**.
   - A pointer back to the **List A inventory row and matrix row**, so that any reinterpretation or additional use remains grounded in the 5.5.1 evidence.

The resulting List B is thus a **transparent, criteria-based subset** of List A, designed to support the Section 3 narrative, the NCAIF content mapping, and the executive-summary framing without re-opening the underlying literature review.
