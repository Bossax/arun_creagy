
### **Project Note: Workshop Strategy & Coordination**

**Date:** March 27, 2026

**Subject:** Bridging the NFCS Baseline to the CRBD Action Plan

---

#### **1. Background & Context**

The current focus is the transition between the baseline data collected by the Thai Meteorological Department (TMD) and the upcoming workshops. The goal is to create a seamless storyline for stakeholders who are participating across multiple sessions led by different agencies (TMD and DCCCE).

#### **2. Core Objectives for April 30th**

- **Build, Don't Repeat:** Ensure the CRBD workshop acts as a "bridge" or a "baton pass" from the TMD/WMO baseline meeting. It must move beyond identifying datasets to synthesizing them.
    
- **Facilitation Shift:** Move away from passive polling and row-seating. The next session requires a more active process to ensure the time spent is valuable for high-level stakeholders.
    
- **Breakout Discussions:** Implement small group sessions to foster deeper engagement and specific outputs that were missing from the previous meeting.
    

#### **3. Strategic Timeline**

- **March:** TMD/WMO meeting (Baseline of climate service products/services).
    
- **April 30:** CRBD Workshop (The Bridge; hosted by DCCCE).
    
- **Late May:** TMD Workshop (Validation and finalization of the NFCS Action Plan).
    

#### **4. Action Items**

- Design breakout session prompts focused on hazard, impact, and risk assessment.
    
- Coordinate with DCCCE to align the narrative with the WMO’s National Framework for Climate Services (NFCS).
    
- Refine the facilitation plan to avoid "boring" formats and maximize stakeholder input.
