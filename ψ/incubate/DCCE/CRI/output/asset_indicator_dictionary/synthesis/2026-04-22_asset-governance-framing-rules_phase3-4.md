# Framing Rules for Linking Asset Indicators to Process-Based Governance (Phase 3/4 Bridge)

## Purpose
This note distills the cross-cutting framing rules from the Phase 1 reread corpus into an operational bridge for Phase 3 concept hardening and Phase 4 Thai anchor scanning.

## Core framing logic (stock-to-process)
1. **Assets are structural potential (hardware), not resilience outcomes**.
   - Asset indicators represent latent capacity or structural floor across SETS stock domains.
   - Interpretation must not treat asset magnitude as realized resilience.

2. **Governance is activation logic (software)**.
   - Institutions, coordination, O&M, budgeting, staffing, and accountability activate stock into function.
   - Process strength is therefore a co-equal interpretation axis, not a downstream add-on.

3. **Realized service flow is the required bridge variable**.
   - Stock (what exists) and governance (how it is operated) must be interpreted against service performance (what is delivered).
   - Asset interpretation without service evidence is incomplete.

## Diagnostic rules for mismatch and activation
4. **Mismatch rule: M = A - G**.
   - High positive mismatch indicates asset accumulation outpacing governance capacity.
   - Interpret as fragility anchor or potential white-elephant risk, not automatic strength.

5. **Activation rule: Q = S/P (used where supportable by source evidence)**.
   - Potential (P) from stock is only meaningful if converted to service (S).
   - Low activation flags under-used, inaccessible, or weakly governed stock.

## Nonlinearity, thresholds, and lock-in
6. **Threshold rule**.
   - Asset-governance relationships are nonlinear; performance can degrade abruptly after governance falls below activation threshold.
   - Early-warning interpretation should watch for widening stock-governance-service gaps.

7. **Lock-in rule**.
   - High-CAPEX, low-modularity assets can narrow future adaptation pathways.
   - Treat rigid infrastructure as conditionally beneficial: potential near-term protection, but long-term liability if flexibility is low.

## Safe-interpretation guardrails (mandatory)
8. **Wealth-bias control**.
   - Normalize asset interpretation against hazard exposure and service dependency; avoid property-value dominance.

9. **Accessibility and distribution control**.
   - Distinguish presence from access; spatial and social distribution are part of indicator meaning.

10. **Ranking-trap control**.
   - Do not rank jurisdictions by stock totals alone.
   - Use paired interpretation: stock level + governance quality + service realization.

11. **SETS-to-Institutional control**.
   - Keep stock categorization (Social, Ecological, Technological) explicitly linked to Institutional activation processes.
   - Asset indicators complement, and do not replace, process-based governance indicators.

## Operational implications for upcoming work
### A) Phase 3 concept hardening
- Keep each asset concept phrased as enabling condition, with explicit activation dependency.
- Add interpretation notes that state expected governance counterpart(s) and likely service-flow manifestation.
- Flag concepts with probable threshold/lock-in behavior for cautious use.

### B) Phase 4 Thai anchor scanning
- Prioritize anchors that allow a three-part read: stock presence, governance/process proxy, and service-performance proxy.
- Add hazard-exposure normalization and accessibility/distribution checks during anchor mapping.
- De-prioritize anchors that only provide raw stock volume without activation or equity context.

## Scope note
This note is an intermediate framing artifact only. It does not perform template audit, clustering, or drafting of downstream Phase 3/4 deliverables.
