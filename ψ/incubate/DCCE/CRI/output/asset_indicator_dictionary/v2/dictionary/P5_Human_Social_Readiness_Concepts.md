| Trust, norms of reciprocity, and degree of connectedness across community groups | Cognitive social capital that enhances social cohesion, mutual support, and information sharing, acting as a crucial informal safety net during crises | Adaptive | Social | "Urban Resilience Assessment - Multiple Dimentions, Criteria, and Indicators.pdf" |
| Population with emergency response and recovery skills (e.g., first aid, diverse pooled skills) | Human assets that empower individuals to take immediate, autonomous lifesaving actions and manage risks locally, supplementing or substituting for formal, centralized emergency responses | Coping | Social | "Urban Resilience Assessment - Multiple Dimentions, Criteria, and Indicators.pdf" |
| Physical and psychological health, and local healthcare capacity (e.g., density of doctors) | Foundational human capital stocks that define a population's intrinsic vulnerability to shocks and the local system's surge capacity to manage health crises during extreme events | Coping | Social | "Urban Resilience Assessment - Multiple Dimentions, Criteria, and Indicators.pdf", "Indicators for Monitoring Urban Climate Change Resilience and Adaptation.pdf" |
| Literacy, education, and hazard knowledge/risk competence | Intellectual capabilities that foster the capacity to learn, process risk information (e.g., heat or flood warnings), internalize past experiences, and adapt livelihoods to changing environmental conditions | Adaptive | Social | "A framework for urban climate resilience.pdf", "Urban Resilience Assessment - Multiple Dimentions, Criteria, and Indicators.pdf", "Indicators for Monitoring Urban Climate Change Resilience and Adaptation.pdf" |
| Empowerment of vulnerable groups and social safety-net mechanisms | Targeted institutional and community support structures designed to reduce the disproportionate impacts of disasters on marginalized populations and actively engage them in planning and recovery | Transformative | Social | "Urban Resilience Assessment - Multiple Dimentions, Criteria, and Indicators.pdf" |
| Equitable access, rights, and entitlements to basic needs, infrastructure, and services (e.g., lifeline tariff structures) | Inclusive governance structures and institutional rules that prevent systematic social marginalization, ensuring all socio-economic groups can affordably access vital lifelines during normal and crisis conditions | Transformative | Social | "A framework for urban climate resilience.pdf", "Urban Resilience Assessment - Multiple Dimentions, Criteria, and Indicators.pdf" |
