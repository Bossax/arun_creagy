## Observations

1.  **The "Big Five" Infrastructure Services**: Water, Energy, Transport, Shelter, and Communications are consistently identified as the core "built" or "technological" pillars across almost all frameworks (1, 4, 5, 6, 7).
2.  **The Ecological Pivot**: Framework 2 and 5 elevate "Natural Areas" or "Ecological Systems" to the same strategic level as physical infrastructure, which aligns with the CRI's goal of integrating nature-based solutions.
3.  **The Socio-Economic Layer**: Framework 3 (World Bank) and Framework 7 (Feldmeyer) add the critical "Human" and "Governance" layers, which are essential for determining the *capacity* of the assets to function under stress.
4.  **Redundancy Check**: Framework 1 and 6 are both Tyler & Moench. Framework 6 provides a more integrated view (e.g., Water and Food supply as one survival network).
