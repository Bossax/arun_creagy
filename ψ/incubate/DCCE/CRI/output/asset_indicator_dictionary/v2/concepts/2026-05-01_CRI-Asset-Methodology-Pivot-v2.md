# Methodology Pivot: From Academic SETS to Service-Oriented Resilience Pillars (CRI v2.0)

## 1. Rationale: Why we Changed (The Adoption Wall)
The original CRI Asset Dictionary (v1.0) was grounded in the academic **SETS (Social-Ecological-Technological Systems)** framework. While theoretically robust, forensic auditing and stakeholder feedback (E-CRI-040) revealed two critical failure points:

*   **Operational Invisible Silos**: Categorizing assets by their "nature" (e.g., a tree as Ecological, a pipe as Technological) forced local officials to search across three academic buckets for a single functional goal. In reality, Thai local government (อปท.) manages by **Functional Mandates (ภารกิจ)** and budget lines.
*   **Linguistic Alienation**: Technical terms like "Ecological Stock" and "Form-Function Stock" created an "adoption wall," making the index feel like an academic artifact rather than an actionable diagnostic tool.

## 2. Exploration: New Approaches to Framing Resilience
To ground the new framework, we explored **7 Global Frameworks** (Source: `notebooklm_raw/`) to identify the "Resilience Delta" between generic urban services and climate resilience systems.

### Key Frameworks Analyzed:
1.  **Critical Urban Systems (Tyler & Moench, 2012)**: Defined "criticality" as systems whose failure precludes higher-order economic activity.
2.  **Strategic Urban Services (Russo et al., 2023)**: Integrated "Natural Areas" and "Waste" as formal strategic services.
3.  **Comprehensive Wealth Portfolio (World Bank, 2024)**: Emphasized the portfolio approach to Produced, Natural, and Human capital.
4.  **Multi-Dimensional Action Fields (Feldmeyer et al., 2019)**: Provided the logical 6-field structure (Environment, Infrastructure, Economy, Society, Governance, Information) that ensures symmetry.

## 3. Selection Criteria for the v2.0 Pillars
The final 6-pillar structure was selected based on four core criteria:

1.  **Symmetry ($M = A \times G$)**: The Asset Pillars must mirror the 6 Governance Pillars to allow for a direct "Multiplicative Resilience" calculation.
2.  **Sufficiency (The Audit)**: The structure must house all "Systemic Gatekeepers" (e.g., Waste) and "Mediators" (e.g., Data) without hiding them in unrelated silos.
3.  **Operational Thai (อปท. Ready)**: Names must reflect the "Resilience Mission" while using vocabulary familiar to municipal directors.
4.  **Resilience Depth**: Each pillar must explicitly address **Coping, Adaptive, and Transformative** capacities.

## 4. The Verdict: The 6 Resilience Missions (Pillars)

The v2.0 framework regroups assets into **6 Mission-Oriented Pillars**. Each pillar is a **"SETS Bundle"** (integrating Social, Ecological, and Technological components) designed to deliver a specific Resilience Outcome.

coping with disaster event
adapt to long term changes
transform to offer new solutions and possibilities
