# CRI Asset Tagging Dictionary v2: Service-Oriented Resilience Stocks
[[ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRI/output/asset_indicator_dictionary/v2/concepts/2026-05-01_CRI-Asset-Methodology-Pivot-v2.1|2026-05-01_CRI-Asset-Methodology-Pivot-v2.1]]

## 1. Objective
This dictionary provides a comprehensive list of "Stock Indicators" for urban resilience, categorized into 6 functional pillars. It is designed to be geographically neutral, supporting Thai Local Administrative Organizations (LAOs) in identifying the physical, ecological, social, and economic assets that enable their communities to cope, adapt, and transform in the face of climate change.

Resilience is measured as $M = A \times G$ (Resilience = Assets × Governance). This dictionary defines the **Asset (A)** component across six missions.

## 2. Detailed Pillar Definitions & Resilience Logic

### Resilience in Action: A Lifecycle Narrative
To understand how these pillars endorse a city's resilience, we trace the narrative of a city facing an extreme climate event (e.g., a severe flood or storm):

*   **Phase 1: Anticipation (P1 & P4)**
    Before the event, the city maintains its daily metabolism. Because the city has invested in **Basic Services (P1)** with redundant energy and water stocks, it doesn't collapse under early stress. Simultaneously, the **Decision Intelligence (P4)** support system detects threshold breaches, providing the data needed to trigger alerts for all other pillars.

*   **Phase 2: Absorption & Response (P2 & P5)**
    When the event hits, the **Security Hardware (P2)** (e.g., pumps, barriers, or check-dams) acts as the first line of defense to absorb the hazard. These are assets specifically built to mediate hazards. If the hardware is overwhelmed, the city's **Social Readiness (P5)** (social capital, volunteer networks) kicks in. Neighbors rescue the vulnerable, substituting human agency for physical hardware.

*   **Phase 3: Elasticity & Recovery (P6)**
    After the waters recede, the city relies on its **Economic Buffer (P6)** (economic capital, emergency funds). This "Financial Stock" injects immediate liquidity, ensuring that markets reopen and homes are repaired quickly, preventing a long-term decline.

*   **Phase 4: Evolution & Transformation (P3)**
    Finally, the city uses its **Spatial Transformation (P3)** assets (e.g., sponge parks or land-use changes) to evolve. This ensures that the next event has less impact, moving the city from "Coping" to "Evolving."

### P1: ระบบบริการพื้นฐานและโครงข่ายสาธารณูปโภค (Basic Services & Utilities)
*   **Role:** Service Continuity (Daily Life).
*   **Definition:** Focuses on the functionalities of basic city services including **Energy, Water, Mobility, and Food**. We lump these together because they represent the primary urban service systems. This category is expected to "bloat" because it contains the dense functional details of city metabolism.
*   **Operational Examples:** Decentralized water storage, micro-grids, multiple access roads, and drainage maintenance status.

### P2: ระบบความมั่นคงและการจัดการภัยพิบัติ (Security & Disaster Management)
*   **Role:** Coping (Short-to-Medium Term).
*   **Definition:** Focuses on hazard mitigation and disaster response. These are the **climate/hazard aspects of basic infrastructure**—assets specifically built to mediate hazards and deal with disaster events, including response and relief.
*   **Operational Examples:** Pumping stations, seawalls, check-dams, landslide barriers, evacuation centers, and emergency equipment.

### P3: ระบบการปรับเปลี่ยนพื้นที่และนิเวศเมือง (Spatial & Ecological Transformation)
*   **Role:** Transformation (Long-Term).
*   **Definition:** Assets that allow the city to change its physical or ecological structure to adapt to long-term climate changes. It focuses on "Safe-to-Fail" and nature-based evolution.
*   **Operational Examples:** Mangrove buffers, protection forests, sponge-parks (detention basins), and permeable urban surfaces.

### P4: ระบบข้อมูลและเทคโนโลยีเพื่อการตัดสินใจ (Data & Decision Intelligence)
*   **Role:** Intelligence (Decision Support).
*   **Definition:** Focuses on the **decision support system** that supports all other pillars. It is the "nervous system" providing situational awareness.
*   **Operational Examples:** Telemetry rainfall stations, CCTV for monitoring, community radio, and GIS-based disaster war rooms.

### P5: ระบบทุนมนุษย์และเตรียมความพร้อมทางสังคม (Human & Social Readiness)
*   **Role:** Readiness (Social Capital).
*   **Definition:** The assets and stocks that represent **social capital**. It tracks the capabilities and organization of the population required to maintain resilience when hardware fails.
*   **Operational Examples:** Trained volunteer networks (อปพร.), community committees, health facility surge capacity, and vulnerable group databases.

### P6: ระบบทุนทางเศรษฐกิจและเสถียรภาพทางการเงิน (Economic & Financial Buffer)
*   **Role:** Buffer (Economic Capital).
*   **Definition:** The assets and stocks that represent **economic capital**. These financial buffers ensure the city has the liquidity to repair and recover without secondary collapse.
*   **Operational Examples:** Municipal disaster funds, insurance penetration, local economic diversity, and low-interest recovery loans.
