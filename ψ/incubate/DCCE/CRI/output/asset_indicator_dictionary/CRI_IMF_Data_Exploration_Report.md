## 1. Executive Summary: The Structural Floor

Analysis of the IMF Investment and Capital Stock Dataset (ICSD) reveals that Thailand has built a massive "Structural Floor" of productive assets. However, the composition of these assets suggests that resilience cannot be achieved by government action alone.

**Key Findings**:
1.  **Private Dominance**: As of 2019, the private sector holds **$2,191.8 Billion (PPP)** in capital stock, compared to the government's **$970.7 Billion**.
2.  **Activation Gap**: While "Hardware" (Capital Stock) has grown steadily, "Investment Velocity" (Capital Formation as % of GDP) has stabilized, suggesting the focus must shift from **building new stocks** to **activating existing ones**.
3.  **PPP Potential**: Public-Private Partnership (PPP) stocks remain a tiny fraction of the total, representing a significant untapped "Software" coordination opportunity.

## 3. Safe Interpretation for CRI

Based on this data, the following guardrails are integrated into the CRI methodology:

1.  **The Mismatch Index ($M = A - G$)**: We must flag sectors where private capital (High $A$) exists in regions with weak local governance (Low $G$). This is where the risk of **Systemic Failure** is highest.
2.  **The Veto-Point Guardrail**: If public capital formation (the Green "Flow") drops below a threshold, it acts as a structural veto on the maintenance of both public and private stocks, leading to **Maladaptive Lock-in**.
