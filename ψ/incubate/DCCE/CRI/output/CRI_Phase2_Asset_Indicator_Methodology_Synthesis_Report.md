# CRI Phase 2 Methodology Synthesis Report: The Asset-Governance Bridge

## 1. Objective and Trigger (E-CRI-030)
Based on stakeholder insights captured on 2026-04-22 (E-CRI-030), the project pivoted to include **Asset-Based (Stock) Indicators** alongside the established **Governance (Process) Pillars**. This serves as a conceptual bridge to move stakeholders from a static "Hardware" view of resilience toward a dynamic "Multiplicative" view.

## 2. Extraction Logic (Stage A)
We performed a deep scan of SETS (Social-Ecological-Technological Systems) scholarship (McPhearson et al., 2022; Kim et al., 2022). 
- **Method**: Systematic extraction of 57 raw "Asset-Indicator Rows" (AIR-001 to AIR-057).
- **Hardening**: These 57 rows were clusters and flattened into **11 Hardened Concepts (HC-01 to HC-11)** to reduce administrative noise while preserving scientific depth.

## 3. The Activation Bridge (Logic Layer)
Resilience is operationally modeled as a multiplicative function:
**$S = A \times \sigma(G)$**
- **A (Hardware)**: The enabling stock (potential).
- **G (Software)**: The institutional readiness (activation).
- **Logic**: Assets remain "dormant" until governance processes put them into use. This logic identifies **Stranded Assets** (high A, zero G) as primary fragility risks.

## 4. Validation and Provenance
To prevent "Expert Drift," a forensic validation pass was executed:
- All "Evidence" columns were audited to ensure they cite **Literature Anchors** rather than internal AI synthesis notes.
- "Process Drift" rows (like AIR-011) were moved to the Governance layer to maintain category purity in the Asset stock inventory.
