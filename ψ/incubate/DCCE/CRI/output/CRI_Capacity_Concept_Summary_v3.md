# CRI Capacity v3 Indicator Concept Summary (Institutional Readiness)

| indicator_concept           | capacity_category | governance_function         | description (summarised)                                                                                                                                                                                                                        | candidate_indicator (high-level)                                                                                                       |
| --------------------------- | ----------------- | --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Plan revision cycle         | Adaptive          | Planning & Strategy         | Frequency and quality of updating core climate/DRM and development plans in light of new risk information, demographic change, and post‑event learning, rather than treating plans as static documents.                                         | Existence and year of latest climate/DRM plan; presence of formal revision schedule or legal requirement for periodic updates.         |
| Risk‑informed planning      | Adaptive          | Planning & Strategy         | Degree to which multi‑hazard risk and vulnerability assessments are embedded in land‑use, infrastructure, and investment planning, using hazard maps and diagnostics to steer siting, zoning, and capital budgeting.                            | Use of risk or hazard maps in strategic/land‑use plans; references to multi‑risk diagnostics in planning documents.                    |
| Policy Integration          | Adaptive          | Planning & Strategy         | Strength of cross‑sectoral and multi‑level mechanisms that align climate resilience objectives across departments and tiers of government, reducing siloed action through shared strategies, data platforms, and participatory processes.       | Presence of cross‑sector resilience strategies; inclusion of climate resilience in multiple sector plans and budgets.                  |
| Emergency budget flow       | Coping            | Finance & Procurement       | Speed, flexibility, and reliability with which funds can be mobilised and disbursed during and after shocks to protect basic services and support recovery, including use of contingency reserves and flexible budget lines.                    | Existence and size of emergency funds; time from disaster declaration to disbursement; share of budget for emergency services.         |
| Climate budget tagging      | Adaptive          | Finance & Procurement       | Existence and use of budget classifications or tagging systems that identify climate‑relevant spending, allowing tracking and reallocation of finance toward resilient investments over time.                                                   | Presence of climate‑related budget codes (e.g. e‑LAAS tags); share of expenditure tagged as climate adaptation/mitigation.             |
| Asset Mobilization Velocity | Coping            | Finance & Procurement       | Conceptual speed at which public, private, and community assets (equipment, facilities, supplies) can be mobilised and deployed to front‑line response; currently a design placeholder awaiting direct evidence.                                | TBD; potential indicators include time to mobilise critical equipment or vehicles; existence of pre‑agreed asset mobilisation SOPs.    |
| Resource allocation scale   | Adaptive          | Finance & Procurement       | Magnitude and coverage of financial resources dedicated to resilience and emergency functions relative to overall budget and risk profile, including presence of dedicated reserves and diversified funding models.                             | % of budget plausibly resilience‑related; presence of dedicated disaster reserves and contingency lines; diversity of funding sources. |
| Multi‑stakeholder collab.   | Adaptive          | Coordination & Partnerships | Density and quality of structured interactions between government, private sector, academia, civil society, and vulnerable communities (e.g. Shared Learning Dialogues) that surface diverse knowledge and jointly steer resilience priorities. | Existence and frequency of multi‑stakeholder resilience forums/committees; documented SLDs or consultation processes.                  |
| Distributed Command Agility | Transformative    | Coordination & Partnerships | Conceptual ability of leadership and command functions to decentralise, delegate, and re‑route decision‑making authority during crises (e.g. SOPs enabling local delegation); currently a design placeholder.                                   | TBD; potential indicators include SOPs for delegated authority, backup incident commanders, or decentralised command centres.          |
| Transboundary Feedbacks     | Adaptive          | Coordination & Partnerships | Conceptual capacity of governance arrangements to recognise and manage cross‑jurisdictional and cross‑scale feedbacks (e.g. upstream–downstream watershed effects); currently awaiting explicit evidence.                                       | TBD; potential indicators include inter‑provincial MoUs on watershed or coastal management; joint plans across administrative units.   |
| Formal coordination bodies  | Transformative    | Coordination & Partnerships | Existence and mandate of formal cross‑departmental working groups, taskforces, or coordination bodies that steer resilience strategy and link fragmented departments and, in some cases, external stakeholders.                                 | Legal or administrative mandate for a resilience/climate risk working group or CRO‑type entity; records of meetings and outputs.       |
| Service delivery timeliness | Adaptive          | Service Delivery & Ops      | Rapidity with which essential climate‑relevant services (emergency response, utilities, social protection) react to and recover from disruptions, including time to first response and time to restore acceptable service levels.               | Average response time for emergency services; duration to restore key services post‑event; continuity of critical operations.          |
| Case throughput rate        | Adaptive          | Service Delivery & Ops      | Rate at which complaints, incidents, or service requests (e.g. Traffy Fondue cases) are processed and resolved; partially evidenced and may need refinement as additional sources are added.                                                    | Number of cases resolved per period; proportion of cases resolved within target time; backlog size relative to inflow.                 |
| Digital Social Mediation    | Adaptive          | Service Delivery & Ops      | Conceptual use of digital platforms, social media, and peer‑to‑peer tools to match needs and resources (e.g. crowdsourced aid‑matching); currently a design placeholder pending explicit literature.                                            | TBD; potential indicators include reach and utilisation of digital aid‑matching platforms; volume of verified reports handled.         |
| Infrastructure robustness   | Absorptive        | Service Delivery & Ops      | Capacity of physical assets and networks (transport, energy, water, social infrastructure) to maintain function or fail safely under shocks, based on design standards, performance under stress, and protective and redundancy measures.       | Share of critical assets built/retrofitted to hazard‑resistant standards; existence of protective infrastructure; redundancy levels.   |
| Data interoperability       | Transformative    | Data & Digital Governance   | Degree to which data can be shared, integrated, and used across departments and sectors via interoperable systems, shared standards, and formal data‑sharing agreements, enabling shared situational awareness.                                 | Existence of integrated e‑government platforms; data‑sharing agreements; availability of consolidated, cross‑sector data portals.      |
| Digital climate services    | Transformative    | Data & Digital Governance   | Deployment of digital tools (EWS, digital twins, real‑time dashboards, geo‑tagged data platforms) to monitor, predict, and communicate climate‑related risks, supporting proactive and participatory risk management.                           | Availability and reliability of early warning apps; operational digital twins; reach of climate risk dashboards/portals.               |
| Risk Assessment depth       | Adaptive          | Data & Digital Governance   | Breadth and sophistication of risk diagnostics, moving from single‑hazard technical studies toward multi‑risk, participatory assessments that capture cascading impacts, systemic fragilities, and institutional constraints.                   | Existence of multi‑risk assessments; inclusion of cascading impact analysis; participatory components in risk studies.                 |
| Emergency staff capacity    | Coping            | HR & Institutional Capacity | Availability, distribution, and competencies of personnel required to manage crises (emergency response teams, technical staff, specialised health personnel), including institutional arrangements for deployment.                             | Number of trained emergency staff per population; existence of dedicated emergency/disaster teams; coverage of key technical skills.   |
| Staff Training frequency    | Adaptive          | HR & Institutional Capacity | Regularity and coverage of training programmes related to resilience, risk management, and emergency operations; currently partially evidenced and should be refined once explicit metrics are available.                                       | Frequency of resilience/DRM trainings per year; share of relevant staff trained; existence of mandatory training requirements.         |
