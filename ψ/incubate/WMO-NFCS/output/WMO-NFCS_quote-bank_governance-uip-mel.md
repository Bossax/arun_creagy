# WMO–NFCS quote bank (governance, UIP/portal, MEL)

**Purpose**: preserve verbatim, citable clauses from WMO NFCS guidance/templates (and a small number of country examples) that help **risk-mitigate** CRDB/NCAIF ↔ NFCS narrative integration.

**Intended use**:
- Steering-facing language that does **not** imply a line agency “hosts” the NFCS action plan
- Sponsor-safe rationale for a TOR-literal **“website blueprint”** by positioning it as part of the NFCS **User Interface Platform (UIP)** concept (where appropriate)

## Theme 2 — UIP / portal / platform (incl. “website blueprint” alignment)

| Quote (verbatim) | Source (doc + section/page if available) | Implication for CRDB/NCAIF narrative (1 line) |
|---|---|---|
| “User interface platform: A structured means of engagement for users, climate researchers and climate service providers to interact.” | Guidelines for Implementing a NFCS, Section 2 | UIP is *not just IT*; it’s an engagement mechanism (protects against “portal-first” misread). |
| “A key purpose of a User Interface Platform (UIP) is to establish timely coordination between users and producers to provide user-driven and user-relevant services, to enable better preparedness for climate risks.” | Annex B – NFCS Action Plan Template, Section 3.2 | Position any NCAIF web blueprint as enabling user–producer coordination and preparedness. |
| “A User Interface Platform (UIP) is a central forum for transforming climate information into sector-tailored, actionable climate services. It is a structured means for all stakeholders to interact and is critical to ensuring the aims of the NFCS are met.” | Annex B – NFCS Action Plan Template, Section 3.2 | Keep steering narrative anchored on “translation into actionable services,” not “data portal.” |
| “The most common UIPs used as part of the NFCS are the National Climate Forum (NCF, sometimes called National Climate Outlook Forum, NCOF), and a website containing relevant information, release of new products and services, and the opportunity to provide feedback or engage in other ways.” | Guidelines for Implementing a NFCS, Section 3, Step 5 | WMO explicitly legitimizes a **website** as part of UIP; strong sponsor-safe bridge to website blueprint deliverable. |
| “The User Interface Platform pillar aims to improve the accessibility and usability of climate information for decision-makers and the public. It involves the development of tools, platforms, and interfaces that allow users from various sectors to easily access and interpret climate data...” | National Framework for Climate Services in Viet Nam, Section 3.1 | Confirms UIP can manifest as multiple tools/interfaces; helps avoid over-promising a single national platform. |
| “UIPs are perhaps the most critical element of the NFCS and therefore should be a priority within the Action Plan once the NFCS is launched, or prior to launch if that is possible.” | Guidelines for Implementing a NFCS, Section 3, Step 5 | Justifies early emphasis on UIP design/resourcing in action planning. |
| “Ensure ownership and leadership from an individual or organisation with the resources to make sure the development and operation take place.” | Guidelines for Implementing a NFCS, Box 6 | Protects against unfunded mandates; clarifies the platform needs a resourced owner/operator. |
| “Make arrangements for how the UIP will be operated and agree on those arrangements with participants, including the format of and approach to engagement.” | Guidelines for Implementing a NFCS, Box 6 | Governance/operating model must be agreed; helps manage stakeholder incentive risk (Toey lens). |
| “User interface platforms can also be established at sector levels (e.g agriculture sector, water sector). Sector User Interface Platforms allow for the customization and tailoring of climate information according to the needs of different users.” | NFCS Kenya Complete Report, Section 4.3 | Supports “service bundle” approach and sector-specific rails; avoids one-size-fits-all national portal promise. |
| “The digital platform will be an interactive online multi-hazard early warning system, accessible to all climate service providers and users. The platform will be characterized by robust web and mobile based functionalities...” | NFCS Kenya Complete Report, Section 4.6 | Optional exemplar that a UIP can include a robust web/mobile platform; use carefully (don’t over-commit). |

## Safe steering-level phrasing (Do / Don’t)

### Do
- Use WMO language: **“oversight”** provides political support/buy-in; **“coordinating”** institution drives development/implementation.
- Frame NFCS as **initiated/led by NMHS** (in many countries), with **inter-ministerial oversight** where applicable.
- Describe UIP as a **structured engagement mechanism** (forums + digital), where a **website can be one UIP element**.
- State that UIP needs **resourced ownership/leadership** and agreed operating arrangements.
- Include MEL as part of NFCS implementation: **SMART activities**, periodic review cadence, and formal reporting within governance structures.

### Don’t
- Don’t say “DCCE hosts the NFCS” or “NFCS action plan belongs to DCCE/TMD” (use oversight/coordinating framing).
- Don’t reduce UIP to “just a portal” (UIP = engagement + translation to actionable services).
- Don’t promise a single national mega-platform if governance and resourcing aren’t agreed.
- Don’t imply MEL is optional “nice to have”; cite WMO wording that it is essential for progress/adjustment.

---

## How to use this in CRDB/NCAIF outputs

1) In steering deck/decision memo: use **Theme 1** quotes to anchor ownership/governance without triggering line-agency politics.
2) In NCAIF TOR-literal annex: label the “website blueprint” explicitly as supporting UIP functions (coordination, dissemination, feedback) per **Theme 2**.
3) In action-plan framing: add a minimal MEL section (SMART actions + review cadence + reporting) using **Theme 3** to legitimize accountability.
